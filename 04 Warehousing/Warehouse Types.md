## Definition

A warehouse is a physical infrastructure for storage of goods with value-added activities to ensure product availability at the right time in desired quantity. Modern warehouses are now called **Fulfilment Centres** — they are a competitive differentiator, not just a storage cost.

> Example: IKEA warehouses serve as showroom + display centre + distribution point — eliminating the retailer leg entirely.

---

## Three Classification Dimensions

### A. Ownership-Based

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Private** | Owned and operated by the company itself; full control, high fixed cost |
| **Public** | Rented space from a third-party operator; flexible, lower commitment |
| **Contract** | Long-term lease with a dedicated provider; balance of control and flexibility |
| **Cooperative** | Shared by a group of businesses (common in agriculture / perishables) |

### B. Function-Based

| Type | Function |
|---|---|
| **Distribution Centre (DC)** | Receives from suppliers, picks and ships to customers / stores — high throughput, short storage |
| **Fulfilment Centre (FC)** | E-commerce focused; individual order picking, packing, last-mile handoff |
| **Consolidation Centre** | Aggregates goods from multiple suppliers before onward shipment |
| **Break-Bulk Centre** | Receives large shipments, breaks into smaller lots for distribution |
| **Cross-Dock** | Transfer only — no storage; inbound sorted and transferred to outbound |
| **Bonded Warehouse** | Government-controlled; imports stored before customs duty payment |
| **Cold Storage** | Temperature-controlled; pharma, food, FMCG |

### C. Technical Requirement-Based

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Ambient** | Standard temperature-controlled environment |
| **Cold Chain** | 2–8°C (pharma), <-18°C (frozen food) |
| **Hazmat** | Chemicals, flammable goods — special safety standards |
| **High-Security** | Jewellery, electronics, valuables |
