# Cross Docking

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.4 · Chopra Ch.3
> **Tags:** #warehousing #crossdock #inventory

---

## Definition

Cross docking is an activity within a warehouse (or distribution centre) where products are shipped with **no or minimal storage time** — goods arrive, are sorted for onward distribution, and leave the same facility with minimal dwell time.

There is no inventory held in the cross-dock — just staging and sorting.

---

## How It Works

```
Inbound Truck → Receiving Dock → Sorting / Transfer → Outbound Dock → Outbound Truck
                                       (no storage)
```

- Inbound in bulk quantities
- Sorted into smaller lots based on destination / customer demand
- Inflow and outflow approximately equal in quantity

---

## Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---|---|
| Higher throughput | No storage = faster processing |
| Better inventory turns | No holding = near-zero inventory |
| No obsolescence | Goods never sit long enough to expire or become obsolete |
| Lower handling cost | Fewer touches = lower labour |
| Reduced warehouse space needed | No storage racks or bulk floor required |

---

## Challenges

| Challenge | Impact |
|---|---|
| Tight synchronisation | Inbound and outbound must be perfectly timed |
| High coordination with suppliers | Suppliers must deliver to strict schedule |
| Limited to fast-moving products | Slow movers cannot cross-dock efficiently |
| Technology requirement | WMS + advanced scheduling needed |

---

## Walmart's Cross-Docking Pioneer

Walmart pioneered cross-docking for consumer goods — products are transferred from inbound supplier trucks to outbound retail store trucks at DCs with minimal or no storage.

**Result:** Inventory held only at stores (not at both stores AND warehouses) → lower inventory cost + higher turns.

---

## Types of Cross-Docking

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Pre-packaged** | Supplier ships store-ready cases — no repacking at DC |
| **Consolidation** | Multiple small supplier shipments combined into one outbound truck |
| **Deconsolidation** | Large inbound shipment broken into smaller outbound deliveries |
| **Opportunistic** | Triggered when inventory in system matches an immediate outbound need |
