## Demand Planning Scenario

**Q: Your top SKU just had an unexpected 40% demand spike. How do you respond?**

Framework answer:
1. **Immediate:** Check current inventory and safety stock position against ROP
2. **Short-term:** Expedite orders from suppliers; check if other DCs have stock for rebalancing
3. **Logistics:** Book express transportation if needed; alert customers of potential delay
4. **Supplier:** Alert supplier of increased demand; check their capacity
5. **Forecast update:** Update demand forecast with the new information; adjust safety stock
6. **Root cause:** Understand why spike occurred — promotion? Competition? Seasonal?
7. **Future:** Improve demand sensing for early warning of similar events

## Supplier Failure Scenario

**Q: Your sole-source supplier of a critical component just went bankrupt. What do you do?**

1. **Assess inventory position:** How many days/weeks of inventory remain?
2. **Identify alternatives:** Emergency sourcing from secondary suppliers; spot market
3. **Notify customers:** Proactive communication with affected customers
4. **Production impact:** Which products are affected; can you prioritise high-margin customers?
5. **Medium-term:** Qualify backup supplier immediately; update supplier risk policy
6. **Long-term:** Implement dual-source policy for all critical components; conduct FMEA

## Cost Reduction Scenario

**Q: Asked to reduce logistics costs by 20% without reducing service levels. How?**

Framework answer:
1. **Analyse current cost structure:** Activity-based costing to identify biggest cost drivers
2. **Route optimisation:** Use scientific methods (Vogel's, software) to optimise routes
3. **Mode mix review:** Can more volume move by rail vs road without lead time impact?
4. **Consolidation:** Combine LTL shipments into FTL; milk run optimisation
5. **Vendor management:** Competitive RFQ; negotiate volume-based rates; performance contracts
6. **Network design:** Are DCs in optimal locations? Are there cross-dock opportunities?
7. **Technology:** TMS implementation for route planning; carrier collaboration
8. **Returns reduction:** Reduce reverse logistics cost by reducing damage and errors

## Inventory Optimisation Scenario

**Q: Inventory turns are 4x. Industry average is 12x. How do you improve?**

1. **Diagnose:** ABC analysis to identify slow movers; FSN analysis for non-movers
2. **Demand side:** Improve forecasting accuracy; implement S&OP
3. **Supply side:** Negotiate shorter lead times with suppliers; reduce LT variability
4. **Policy:** Review safety stock levels — may be too high; reduce order quantities
5. **Clearance:** Liquidate non-moving (N-class) items immediately
6. **Postponement:** Delay product differentiation to reduce finished goods variety
7. **Cross-docking:** Implement for fast-moving items to bypass warehouse storage

## Network Design Scenario

**Q: You're opening a new DC. How do you decide where to locate it?**

NEAD-R framework:
1. **Need Assessment:** Throughput, SKU type, temperature needs, customer geography
2. **Evaluate Options:** Survey locations; proximity to customers/suppliers/highways/ports
3. **Analyse:** Cost-benefit (build/lease/3PL); labour market; regulatory compliance
4. **Decide:** Weighted scoring model; total landed cost from each location to customers
5. **Review:** Post-implementation KPI review

Key factors: Customer service radius, inbound transport from suppliers, labour cost and availability, real estate cost, regulatory environment (GST, zoning).
