> **Tags:** #interview #prep #scm

---

## Foundational Questions

| Question | Key Points to Cover |
|---|---|
| What is supply chain management? | All parties involved in fulfilling customer request; 3 flows (product, info, fund); goal = maximise SC surplus |
| What is the difference between logistics and supply chain? | Logistics ⊂ Supply chain; logistics = physical flow execution; SC = full network + strategy |
| Explain push vs pull systems | Push = forecast-driven; pull = demand-driven; push-pull boundary; examples |
| What are the three decision phases in SC? | Strategic (3-10yr), Planning (quarterly-annual), Operational (daily-weekly) |
| What is supply chain surplus? | Customer value − SC cost; only customer = revenue source; focus on total surplus not individual stage |
| What is strategic fit? | Alignment between competitive strategy and SC capabilities; 3 steps to achieve |
| Name and explain the 6 supply chain drivers | Facilities, Inventory, Transportation (logistical); Information, Sourcing, Pricing (cross-functional) |
| What is the bullwhip effect? | Demand variability amplification upstream; 4 causes; mitigation strategies |

## Inventory Questions

| Question | Key Points |
|---|---|
| What is EOQ and its assumptions? | √(2DS/H); constant demand, LT, instant replenishment; limitations |
| What is safety stock? | Buffer against demand/supply uncertainty; formula Z×σ×√LT; factors |
| What is the reorder point? | (Avg demand × LT) + Safety stock; under uncertainty |
| Explain ABC analysis | Pareto principle; A=20% items 80% revenue; B=30%/15%; C=50%/5% |
| What is the difference between Q and P replenishment? | Fixed qty vs fixed interval; perpetual vs periodic count; different SS requirements |
| What is inventory holding cost? | Capital + space + insurance + obsolescence + shrinkage |
| How do you improve inventory turns? | Better forecasting, reduce LT, cross-docking, JIT, ABC rationalisation |

## Logistics and Warehousing

| Question | Key Points |
|---|---|
| Compare transportation modes | Cost vs speed vs reach vs reliability matrix; selection based on priority |
| What is the difference between 3PL and 4PL? | 3PL executes logistics; 4PL manages entire SC including 3PLs |
| How would you design a warehouse? | I/U/L flow; zones; throughput vs space vs resource tradeoff |
| What is cross-docking? | Transfer without storage; benefits: high turns, no obsolescence; challenges: tight synchronisation |
| What metrics do you track in a warehouse? | Picking accuracy, space utilisation, units/hour, order processing time, damage % |
| What is the difference between transit time and lead time? | Transit time ⊂ lead time; lead time = planning to serve including returns |

## Forecasting and Planning

| Question | Key Points |
|---|---|
| What forecasting methods do you know? | Qualitative (Delphi, market research, SFO); quantitative (regression, exponential smoothing, winter's) |
| What is MAPE and when is it useful? | Mean absolute percentage error; scale-free comparison; problematic near zero demand |
| What is S&OP? | Monthly cross-functional process to align demand and supply plans |
| How do you handle seasonality in forecasting? | Seasonal index; Winter's method; aggregate and deseasonalise |

## Lean and Six Sigma

| Question | Key Points |
|---|---|
| What are the 8 wastes of lean? | TIMWOODS — Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Overprocessing, Defects, Skills |
| Explain DMAIC | Define, Measure, Analyse, Improve, Control — Six Sigma improvement methodology |
| What is Poka-Yoke? | Error proofing; shut-out, control, attention strategies |
| What is FMEA? | Failure Mode Effects Analysis; RPN = O×S×D; prioritise high RPN |
| What is JIT? | Just-in-time; materials arrive exactly when needed; requires reliable supply |
