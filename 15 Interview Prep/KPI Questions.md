## "How do you measure supply chain performance?"

**Framework answer:**
- Use a multi-dimensional KPI framework: Quality, Responsiveness, Cost, Productivity
- Kuldeepak's PCS+TGT: Perfect Order, C2C, SC Cycle Time + TEI, GMROI, TSCC
- Chopra's financial: Inventory Turns, C2C, ROA, GMROI

---

## Key KPI Definitions with Formulas

| KPI | Formula | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| **Inventory Turns** | COGS / Avg Inventory | How fast inventory moves; higher = better |
| **Cash-to-Cash Cycle** | DIO + DSO − DPO | Working capital efficiency; negative is best |
| **GMROI** | Gross Profit / Avg Inv × 100 | Profit per ₹ of inventory invested |
| **Turn-Earn Index** | Inv Turns × GP% × 100 | Balanced inventory productivity |
| **Perfect Order Rate** | (Total − Erratic) / Total × 100 | Overall execution quality |
| **Fill Rate** | Shipped / Ordered × 100 | % demand served from available inventory |
| **On-Time Delivery** | On-time / Total × 100 | Logistics service level |
| **Total SC Cost** | Material + Labour + Holding + Logistics + OH | End-to-end cost visibility |

## Tricky KPI Questions

**Q: Amazon had negative C2C. How? Is that good?**
A: Collects from customers before paying suppliers. Amazon's APT = 2.48 (pays every 21 weeks). Yes, very good — suppliers are financing Amazon's operations.

**Q: If inventory turns increase, what happens to C2C?**
A: DIO decreases (365/higher turns) → C2C improves (decreases). Positive impact.

**Q: What is the difference between fill rate and perfect order rate?**
A: Fill rate = fraction of units shipped; Perfect order rate = fraction of orders that are on-time, in-full, undamaged, and correctly invoiced. Perfect order is much harder to achieve.

**Q: Why might a company with high inventory turns still have poor GMROI?**
A: Low gross margin. GMROI = Turns × Margin. If margin is very low, even high turns won't produce good GMROI.
