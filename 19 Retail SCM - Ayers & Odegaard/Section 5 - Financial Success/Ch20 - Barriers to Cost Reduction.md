## Root Causes of Supply Chain Cost

Before addressing cost, identify root causes:

| Root Cause Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Demand variability** | Forecast errors drive safety stock; promotions cause peaks |
| **Lead time variability** | Inconsistent supplier delivery forces safety stock |
| **Process inefficiency** | Manual steps; redundant handling; rework |
| **Poor collaboration** | Partners operate independently; each builds buffer |
| **SKU proliferation** | Too many SKUs = complexity, lower turns, higher handling cost |
| **Returns rate** | High returns = reverse logistics cost; lost margin |

## Four Barriers to Cost Reduction

### 1. No Focus
- Companies have too many SC improvement initiatives simultaneously
- Resources are spread thin; nothing gets done well
- **Fix:** Apply project management discipline — prioritise top 3 initiatives; assign dedicated team; stage-gate process

### 2. Confusion
- People don't understand the SC strategy or why cost matters
- "Promoting SCM" = selling the concept internally before expecting change
- **Graduated approach:** Start with pilots; demonstrate ROI; build internal credibility

### 3. Motivators Misaligned
- Buyers are measured on gross margin %; not inventory turns or SC cost
- Warehouse managers are measured on cost/unit; not service level
- **Fix:** Redesign incentives to align with SC outcomes:
  - Buyers: measure on sell-through %, markdown %, turn-earn index
  - DC managers: measure on fill rate AND cost
  - Suppliers: measure on OTIF AND quality

### 4. Rigidity
- "We've always done it this way" mindset
- Changing processes requires changing habits, systems, and relationships
- **Mindset change tools:** PDCA, DMAIC, benchmarking, external case studies
- **Changing the project:** When a pilot fails, change the approach (not the goal)

## Flexibility vs Rigidity

| Flexible Organisation | Rigid Organisation |
|---|---|
| Measures flexibility as a KPI | Measures stability/predictability only |
| Redesigns processes when SC changes | Defends existing processes |
| Shares data with partners | Guards data as competitive intelligence |
| Tries new SC models (e.g., VMI, CPFR) | Sticks to traditional buyer-supplier model |

---

## Boundaries and Barriers

| Boundary Type | How It Blocks SC Improvement |
|---|---|
| **Functional boundary** | Buying and logistics don't speak; each optimises locally |
| **Company boundary** | Retailer and supplier don't share data; each forecasts independently |
| **Geographic boundary** | Different regions use different systems; no consolidated view |
| **Technology boundary** | Legacy systems can't connect; data trapped in silos |

**"Divide and Conquer" approach:** Break the SC into manageable segments; address one boundary at a time; don't try to fix everything simultaneously.
