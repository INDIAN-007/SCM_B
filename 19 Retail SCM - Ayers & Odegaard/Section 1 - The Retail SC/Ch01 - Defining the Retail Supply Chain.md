# Ch 1 — Defining the Retail Supply Chain

> **Source:** Ayers & Odegaard, Chapter 1
> **Tags:** #retail #definition #SC-model #Ayers

---

## The Retail SC Reference Model

Ayers & Odegaard define the retail supply chain with 7 layers (right to left, i.e., upstream to customer):

```
2nd-Tier Suppliers → 1st-Tier Suppliers → OEMs → Distributors → Retailers → Customers/End-Users
                                                         ↑
                                                  Service Providers (logistics, finance, IT)
```

**Key insight — The Iceberg Analogy:**
> Consumers only see the last transaction (the retailer shelf). The vast majority of the supply chain — raw material sourcing, manufacturing, distribution — is hidden beneath the surface.

---

## What Retailers Do (Levy & Weitz)

Retailers perform four activities more efficiently than OEMs:
1. **Provide assortment** — multiple brands/products in one place (breadth)
2. **Break bulk** — buy in large quantities, sell in small units
3. **Hold inventory** — buffer stock so customers don't wait
4. **Provide services** — credit, returns, product information, convenience

---

## Supply Chain Flows — Three Dimensions

| Flow | Direction | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical flow** | Forward (upstream → customer) | Products, raw materials, packaging |
| **Information flow** | Both ways | Orders, forecasts, POS data, shipment notifications |
| **Financial flow** | Both ways | Payments, credit terms, returns credits |
| **Reverse flow** | Backward | Returns, recalls, recycling |
| **Knowledge flow** | Both ways | Product specs, design inputs, market insights |

---

## Vertical Integration in Retail

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Forward integration** | Manufacturer opens retail stores | Tommy Bahama, Apple Store, Eileen Fisher |
| **Backward integration** | Retailer moves into manufacturing | Zara (owns factories), Marks & Spencer (own-label) |
| **Fully vertically integrated** | Controls full chain from raw material to shelf | IKEA (design, sourcing, manufacturing, retail) |

## Five Tasks for Supply Chain Excellence (Preview)

Ayers & Odegaard identify 5 tasks retail SCM must perform (expanded in Ch 14):
1. Design supply chains for strategic advantage
2. Implement collaborative relationships
3. Manage supply chain partnerships
4. Manage supply chain information
5. Make money from the supply chain
