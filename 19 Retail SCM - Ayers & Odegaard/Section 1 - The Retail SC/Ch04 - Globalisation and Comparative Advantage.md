## Comparative Advantage in Retail SCM

**Comparative advantage** = a country or region can produce a good at lower opportunity cost than others.

Ayers & Odegaard apply this to supply chain design decisions:
- **Why source from China?** Lower labour cost = comparative advantage in labour-intensive manufacturing
- **Why near-shore?** Speed and flexibility = comparative advantage in responsive, trend-sensitive categories

---

## Concept of Distance

"Distance" is not just physical — it has multiple dimensions:

| Dimension | Impact on SCM |
|---|---|
| **Geographic distance** | Longer lead times, higher transport cost |
| **Time zone distance** | Communication lag, slower problem resolution |
| **Cultural distance** | Different business norms, quality expectations |
| **Administrative distance** | Tariffs, customs, compliance requirements |
| **Economic distance** | Wage differential, infrastructure quality |

**Key insight:** Low labour cost (economic advantage) can be offset by cultural, administrative, and geographic distance costs.

---

## Cost Framework for Sourcing Decisions

| Cost Category | Offshore | Nearshore | Domestic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Labour cost** | Lowest | Medium | Highest |
| **Fixed cost (facilities)** | Low–Medium | Medium | High |
| **Purchased item cost** | Low | Medium | Varies |
| **Transport cost** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Lead time cost (inventory carrying)** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Quality risk cost** | Can be high | Medium | Low |
| **Total landed cost** | Often still lower | Competitive | Premium |

---

## When Comparative Advantage Shifts

- Rising wages in China → Vietnam, Bangladesh, India gaining advantage
- Automation reduces labour cost advantage → near-shoring becomes competitive again
- Geopolitical risk (tariffs, sanctions) → diversification essential
- Sustainability pressures → carbon cost of long-distance shipping factored in

---

## Applying the Framework

**Step 1:** Map all cost components (revenue, labour, fixed, purchased items)
**Step 2:** Quantify each for offshore vs nearshore vs domestic
**Step 3:** Add risk cost and lead time cost (safety stock × holding cost)
**Step 4:** Compare total landed cost, not just unit cost
