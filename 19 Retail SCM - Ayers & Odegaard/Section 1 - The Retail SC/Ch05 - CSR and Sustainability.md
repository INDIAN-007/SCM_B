## CSR in Retail — Why It Matters

Retailers are the customer-facing end of the supply chain. Any CSR failure — labour violations, chemical emissions, carbon footprint — reaches the consumer directly and damages the brand.

> "Today's frontier expectations can turn into tomorrow's formal and informal social contract."

---

## CSR Classification Framework (Ayers & Odegaard)

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Type 1 — Good Citizen** | Comply with laws and norms | Pension plan obligations, legal compliance |
| **Type 2 — Mitigation** | Reduce harm proactively | Emission reduction, recycling programs |
| **Type 3 — Strategic Process** | CSR integrated into business strategy | IKEA's sustainability-driven sourcing |
| **Type 4 — Strategic Philanthropy** | Use company capability to improve competitive context | AMD's microprocessor for developing countries |

**Key principle:** Only Types 3 and 4 create competitive advantage. Types 1 and 2 are table stakes.

## Boots Ltd — CSR/Financial Report Convergence

Boots (UK pharmacy retailer) is cited as a leader in integrating CSR reporting with financial reporting:

| CSR Initiative | Category | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Energy-efficient transport | Environmental | -22.9% CO2 per £M sales |
| Double-deck trailers | Environmental | More goods per truck trip |
| Backhaul use (delivering to stores + picking up supplier goods on return) | Operational + Environmental | Reduced empty miles |
| Skills management / lifelong learning | Social | Employee retention |
| Supplier human rights screening | Social/Governance | Reputational risk reduction |

**5 Boots "strategic pillars":** Trust, Ethics, Environment, Community, People

## SCM Links to CSR

| SCM Decision | CSR Implication |
|---|---|
| Supplier selection | Labour practices, environmental compliance at supplier |
| Transportation mode | Carbon footprint (sea vs air vs road) |
| Packaging design | Recyclability, material reduction |
| Returns management | Diversion from landfill, refurbishment options |
| Inventory reduction | Less waste, less obsolescence, less disposal |
| Near-shoring | Reduced shipping emissions, improved labour oversight |
