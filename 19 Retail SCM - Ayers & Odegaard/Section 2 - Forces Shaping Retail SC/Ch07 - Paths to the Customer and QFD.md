## 7 SCM Dimensions for Meeting Market Needs

| # | Dimension | What It Asks |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Product portfolio** | Do we have the right products? |
| 2 | **Product design** | Are products designed for the supply chain (DFM, DFS)? |
| 3 | **Capabilities and capacity** | Can we produce/deliver at required volume and quality? |
| 4 | **Competitive responses** | How does our SC respond when competitors change their offer? |
| 5 | **Product channels** | Are we using the right route to the customer? |
| 6 | **Customer risk concerns** | Are we managing the risks our customers care about? |
| 7 | **Matching supply and demand** | Are we balancing supply capability with demand variability? |

## Nature of Demand — Key Variables

Demand in retail is shaped by:

| Variable | Impact on SC |
|---|---|
| **Volume** | Scale of operations, warehouse size, transport frequency |
| **Variety** | SKU count, picking complexity, supplier diversity |
| **Variability** | Safety stock requirements, capacity buffers |
| **Velocity** | Speed of replenishment, lean vs buffer approach |
| **Visibility** | Point-of-sale data sharing; demand sensing capability |

## Quality Function Deployment (QFD) for SCM

**QFD** = a structured method to translate the Voice of the Customer (VoC) into supply chain requirements.

### QFD Steps:
1. **List customer needs** (the "WHATs") — e.g., product availability, fast delivery, low price
2. **List SC capabilities** (the "HOWs") — e.g., safety stock levels, supplier lead time, DC locations
3. **Build relationship matrix** — score how each HOW addresses each WHAT
4. **Correlation matrix (the "roof")** — identify conflicts between HOWs (e.g., low cost vs high service)
5. **Prioritise HOWs** — focus SC investment on highest-impact capabilities

### Example — Supermarket QFD

| Customer Need (WHAT) | Safety Stock | Supplier OTD | DC Proximity | Promo Planning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Always in stock | Strong | Strong | Medium | Strong |
| Fresh products | Medium | Strong | Strong | Weak |
| Competitive price | Weak | Medium | Weak | Strong |

## Procter & Gamble Case Study (Ch 7)

P&G used SCM dimensions to redesign its fulfilment centres (FCs):
- **Old model:** Functional DCs shipping to retailers
- **New model:** Customer-specific FCs with order tracking, technical support, customer billing
- **Result:** Treated different retailer segments differently; tailored SC capabilities to their needs
- **Lesson:** One SC does not fit all customers — segment your chain
