## Risk Categories (Ayers & Odegaard)

### A. Location / Trading Partner Selection Risks

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| Intellectual property violations | Partner copies design or shares proprietary info |
| Product counterfeiting | Fake goods enter the supply chain |
| Contracting / legal issues | Unclear contracts; unenforceable in some jurisdictions |
| Insufficient oversight | Audit failures at supplier sites |
| Financial difficulty | Supplier goes bankrupt; supply interrupted |
| Technical capability weakness | Supplier cannot consistently meet spec |
| Unknown misaligned incentives | Supplier optimises for their goals, not yours |

### B. External Production / Logistics Risks

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| Foreign exchange reversal | Currency moves wipe out cost advantage |
| Labour disruptions | Strikes, labour shortages at supplier or DC |
| Weather consequences | Hurricane Katrina example — products destroyed or diverted |
| Information infrastructure weakness | System outages disrupt ordering and tracking |
| Material availability | Raw material shortage (e.g., semiconductor, palm oil) |
| Environmental liability | Supplier's practices create reputational/legal risk |

### C. Internal Production / Logistics Risks

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| Market misses | Wrong product, wrong timing — demand forecasting failure |
| Insufficient production capacity | Cannot scale up to meet demand spike |
| Quality performance weakness | Defects escape to retail; customer returns, recalls |
| Synchronisation failures | Partners out of step — too early or too late |
| Forecast error consequences | Overstocking or stockouts due to bad forecast |
| Physical bottlenecks | Dock capacity, transport capacity, DC throughput |
| Delivery variation (lead time) | Inconsistent lead times force higher safety stock |
| Returns handling capability missing | Returns pile up, customer satisfaction falls |

---

## Risk Response Framework

| Response | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Avoidance** | Don't source from high-risk location | When risk probability × impact is very high |
| **Mitigation** | Reduce probability or impact | Buffer stock, dual sourcing, insurance |
| **Transfer** | Pass risk to another party | Insurance, supplier contracts, 3PL SLA |
| **Acceptance** | Live with the risk, monitor | Low probability, low impact events |

---

## The Synchronisation Failure Problem

> "Synchronisation failures and delivery variation will affect smooth running of the supply chain, especially if the chain is tightly linked."

- JIT / Lean chains are MOST vulnerable to synchronisation failures
- A rhythm (daily, weekly, monthly replenishment cycle) across all partners reduces variability
- West Marine (Ch 15) solved this by synchronising its entire supplier base to a common replenishment cycle
