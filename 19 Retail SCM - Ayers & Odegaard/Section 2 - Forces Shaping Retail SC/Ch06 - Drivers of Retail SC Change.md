## Seven Drivers of Retail SC Change

Ayers & Odegaard identify 7 forces that continuously reshape retail supply chains:

| # | Driver | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Innovation Driver** | New product/process technology (external) — companies must innovate or lose relevance |
| 2 | **Extended Product Design** | Products now include services, information, experiences — not just physical goods |
| 3 | **Globalisation** | Offshore sourcing, global competition, country risk |
| 4 | **Flexibility Imperative** | The ultimate capability — the ability to reconfigure the chain faster than competitors |
| 5 | **Process-Centred Management** | Move from functional silos to cross-functional, cross-company processes |
| 6 | **Collaboration** | Multi-company SC coordination is no longer optional — it is a competitive requirement |
| 7 | **Information** | Real-time data from POS to supplier enables demand-driven chains |

---

## The Flexibility Imperative

> "Flexibility is the ultimate capability."

Two dimensions:
1. **Management Mindset** — willingness to redesign processes, not defend existing ones
2. **Defining Needed Flexibility** — what specifically must be flexible?

| Flexibility Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Volume flexibility** | Ability to scale up/down with demand |
| **Mix flexibility** | Ability to change product assortment quickly |
| **Delivery flexibility** | Ability to meet different customer lead time requirements |
| **Launch flexibility** | Speed of bringing new products to market |

## Process-Centred Management vs Functional Silos

| Old Model (Functional) | New Model (Process-Centred) |
|---|---|
| Buying, logistics, store ops work in silos | Cross-functional teams own end-to-end processes |
| Metrics optimised per function | Metrics aligned to customer outcome |
| Information stops at department boundaries | Information flows across functions and companies |
| Blame culture when SC fails | Shared accountability across functions |

---

## Three Stages of Collaboration (SCM Maturity)

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **Stage 1 — Internal** | Improve within the company; functional collaboration |
| **Stage 2 — Dyadic** | Collaborate with one key trading partner (supplier or customer) |
| **Stage 3 — Multicompany** | Multiple trading partners across the chain share data, plans, and risk |

Most companies are at Stage 1 or 2. Stage 3 is the competitive frontier.
