# Ch 11 — Product Types: Functional vs Innovative

> **Source:** Ayers & Odegaard, Chapter 11
> **Tags:** #retail #product-types #functional #innovative #market-mediation #Ayers

---

## Why Product Type Drives SC Design

Different products require fundamentally different supply chains. A one-size-fits-all SC approach leads to either:
- Over-investment (too much stock for predictable products)
- Under-investment (too little buffer for unpredictable products)

---

## Functional vs Innovative Products (Fisher, 1997)

| Attribute | **Functional / Staple** | **Innovative / Fashion** |
|---|---|---|
| Demand | Predictable, stable | Unpredictable, volatile |
| Product life cycle | Long (years) | Short (months, seasons) |
| Margin | Low | High |
| Variety | Low | High |
| Stockout cost | Low–Medium | Very High (missed season) |
| Markdown cost | Low | Very High |
| Replenishment cycle | Continuous (ROP-based) | One-time or test-and-repeat |
| Examples | Flour, salt, basic t-shirts, toiletries | Designer clothing, new electronics, seasonal baking items |

---

## Market Mediation Costs

**Market mediation cost** = cost of mismatch between supply and demand

- **Too much stock:** Markdown losses, storage cost, obsolescence
- **Too little stock:** Stockout, lost sales, customer dissatisfaction

| Product Type | Physical SC Cost | Market Mediation Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional** | Focus on efficiency | Low (demand is predictable) |
| **Innovative** | Responsiveness needed | High (demand is unpredictable) |

**Key principle:** For innovative products, market mediation cost > physical logistics cost. Being responsive (fast, flexible) is more valuable than being cheap.

---

## Product Life Cycle and SC Strategy

| PLC Stage | Demand Pattern | SC Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Introduction** | Low, building | Small batch; test demand; near-source for speed |
| **Growth** | Rising fast | Increase capacity; lock in supply; build inventory |
| **Maturity** | Stable, predictable | Efficiency focus; lean SC; offshore for cost |
| **Decline** | Falling | Reduce orders; liquidate stock; end-of-life planning |

## Application to Your Projects

| Project | Product Type | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Hotel (F&B)** | Mix — fresh (innovative-like) + dry goods (functional) | Two different SC approaches needed |
| **Asian Paints** | Mix — basics/white (functional) + trend colours (innovative) | Base paint = lean; specialty = responsive |
| **Phoon Huat** | Mix — staple baking (functional) + seasonal/festive items (innovative) | Festive items need responsive buying; staples need EOQ |

---

## The "Wrong SC" Problem

Retailers often apply a functional SC design to innovative products:
- Long lead times → miss the trend
- High SS → stuck with unsold stock
- Centralised DC → slow to respond

**Fix:** Identify your innovative products; build a fast, responsive chain for them. Keep the efficient chain only for your functional products.
