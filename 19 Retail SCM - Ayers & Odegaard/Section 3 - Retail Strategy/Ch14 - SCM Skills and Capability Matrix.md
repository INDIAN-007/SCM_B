## Five Tasks for Retail SCM Excellence

| # | Task | Description | Functions Involved |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Design supply chains for strategic advantage** | SC innovation must support competitive strategy | Senior management, merchandising, marketing, finance |
| 2 | **Implement collaborative relationships** | Internal organisational design; cross-functional ownership of processes | All functions |
| 3 | **Manage supply chain partnerships** | External relationships with suppliers, 3PLs, retail partners | Procurement, logistics, category management |
| 4 | **Manage supply chain information** | Technology selection, data governance, analytics | IT, finance, operations |
| 5 | **Make money from the supply chain** | Cost reduction; financial performance linked to SC decisions | Finance, CFO, SC leadership |

## Retail SCM Capability Maturity Matrix

| Task | Level 1 (Ad-hoc) | Level 2 (Defined) | Level 3 (Managed) | Level 4 (Optimised) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SC Design** | No formal SC strategy; reactive | SC strategy documented; functional alignment | SC design reviewed annually with strategy | SC continuously redesigned; competitive differentiator |
| **Collaboration** | Siloed functions; no cross-functional process | Some cross-functional teams; limited partner collaboration | Stage 2 collaboration with key partners | Stage 3 multicompany collaboration across chain |
| **Partnership Management** | Transactional buying only | Key supplier relationships defined | Supplier scorecards; joint improvement programs | Fully integrated SC partnerships; shared risk/reward |
| **Information Management** | Spreadsheets; disconnected systems | ERP implemented; basic reporting | Real-time visibility across functions | Demand-driven; AI-assisted decision making |
| **Financial Performance** | SC cost not tracked | SC cost tracked by function | SC cost tracked end-to-end; linked to P&L | SC decisions modelled for financial impact; ABM used |

**How to use:** Assess your current state per task. Identify the gap to Level 3 or 4. Prioritise based on which task drives your strategic driving force.

## Key Skills for Retail SCM Professionals

| Skill Area | Why Important |
|---|---|
| **Financial analysis** | Link SC decisions to P&L and balance sheet (DuPont model) |
| **Demand forecasting / statistics** | Core tool for inventory optimisation |
| **Process mapping and improvement** | PDCA, DMAIC, CPFR — drive continuous improvement |
| **Supplier management** | Negotiate, develop, and monitor suppliers |
| **Technology literacy** | WMS, TMS, ERP, demand sensing tools |
| **Collaboration skills** | Multi-company SC requires negotiation and trust |
| **Data analytics** | SQL, Python, Excel — analyse SC performance data |

## RILA Retail Supply Chain Certification

The Retail Industry Leaders Association (RILA) offers an SCM certification covering:
- Statistics and forecasting
- Inventory management
- Transportation management
- Supplier relationship management
- Technology systems
- Financial analysis
