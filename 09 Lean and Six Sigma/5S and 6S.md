# 5S and 6S

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.6
> **Tags:** #lean #5s #workplace #organization

---

## Definition

5S is a Japanese workplace organisation methodology for maximising efficiency and reducing idle time due to poor floor management.

---

## The 5S Framework

| S | Japanese | English | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1S** | Seiri | Sort | Remove unnecessary items from the workspace |
| **2S** | Seiton | Set in Order | Organise remaining items — a place for everything |
| **3S** | Seiso | Shine | Clean the workspace; identify cleaning as inspection |
| **4S** | Seiketsu | Standardise | Create standards to maintain the first 3S |
| **5S** | Shitsuke | Sustain | Make 5S a habit; build into culture |

---

## 6S — Adding Safety

Some organisations add a **6th S** for **Safety**:
- Evaluate safety risks at each step
- Integrate safety checks into standard work

---

## 5S in Supply Chain / Warehousing

| Area | 5S Application |
|---|---|
| **Sort** | Remove expired, damaged, obsolete stock; clear clutter from aisles |
| **Set in Order** | Label bins and racks; define locations for all items; floor markings |
| **Shine** | Daily cleaning of floor, equipment, shelving; check for leaks or damage |
| **Standardise** | SOPs for receiving, putaway, picking; visual management boards |
| **Sustain** | Regular 5S audits; shift handover checklist; management involvement |

---

## Benefits

- Reduced time wasted searching for items (motion waste eliminated)
- Safer work environment
- Faster onboarding of new staff (visual management)
- Early detection of equipment problems (cleaning = inspection)
- Foundation for all other lean improvements
