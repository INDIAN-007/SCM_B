# Kaizen and Continuous Improvement

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.6
> **Tags:** #lean #kaizen #improvement

---

## Definition

**Kaizen** = Kai (change) + Zen (good) → "Change for the better"

A philosophy of **continuous, incremental improvement** involving everyone at every level of the organisation — not just managers or engineers.

Key principle: *Utilise the mind-hours of every person involved and challenge the status quo.*

---

## Five Principles of Kaizen (Kaizen Institute)

1. **Know Your Customer** — understand what the customer values
2. **Target Zero Waste** — identify and eliminate all 8 wastes (TIMWOODS)
3. **Be Transparent** — share data, problems, and progress openly
4. **Empower People** — give employees authority to identify and fix problems
5. **Go to GEMBA** — be at the place of action (shop floor, warehouse, delivery point)

---

## What Kaizen Targets

| Japanese | Meaning | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **MUDA** | Waste | Non-value-adding activities (TIMWOODS) |
| **MURA** | Unevenness | Fluctuation in production or demand |
| **MURI** | Overburden | Unreasonable demands on people or machines |

---

## Kaizen Event (Kaizen Blitz)

A focused, time-bound (3–5 day) improvement workshop targeting a specific process:
- Cross-functional team
- Measure current state
- Identify waste and root causes
- Implement improvements immediately
- Measure and document results

---

## 3G Strategy — Gemba, Gembitsu, Genjitsu

| Japanese | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Gemba** | "Actual place" | Go to the shop floor / point of action to observe |
| **Gembitsu** | "Actual thing" | Focus on the actual product, machine, or process |
| **Genjitsu** | "Actual fact" | Incorporate ground reality into decisions |

*Note: The 3G strategy ensures hands-on approach before decision making — avoids perception-based management.*

## Related Continuous Improvement Tools

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Value Stream Mapping (VSM)** | Visualise entire value stream; identify waste |
| **5S / 6S** | Organise workspace; eliminate motion waste |
| **SMED** | Reduce changeover time; increase flexibility |
| **Poka-Yoke** | Error-proofing; prevent defects |
| **FMEA** | Proactively identify and rank risks |
| **Andon** | Visual alarm system to signal production problems |
| **Jidoka** | Autonomation — machines stop automatically on defect detection |
| **Heijunka** | Production levelling — reduce MURA |

---

## Andon (Kuldeepak)

Visual alarm system — communicates quality status via:
- Text messages, email alerts, alarms, lights, visual boards
- Signals when deviation from target occurs
- Stops production until corrected (ties to Jidoka)

Example: Printer with empty toner → red light activates → stops printing

## Jidoka (Kuldeepak)

- Emphasises **elimination of the root cause** before resuming the process
- Detection should be autonomous
- No further build of defective stock until corrected
- Inbuilt quality — not inspection after production

## Heijunka — Production Levelling (Kuldeepak)

- Works toward **"batch size of one"** (true pull)
- Uses SMED, OTED, Takt Time, Kanban to achieve levelling
- Minimises MURA (unevenness) in production
- Reduces excess inventory caused by batch production
