# Pareto Analysis

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.6

---

## Definition

Pareto Analysis (also called the **80/20 rule**, the **Law of Vital Few**) states that **80% of effects come from 20% of causes**.

Introduced by Italian economist Vilfredo Pareto (1906); adapted for industrial quality by Dr. Joseph Juran (1940s).

---

## Formula / Application

1. Collect data on the frequency or impact of each category
2. Sort categories from highest to lowest
3. Calculate cumulative percentage
4. Draw Pareto chart (bar + cumulative line)
5. Focus improvement on the top 20% of causes = 80% of the problem

---

## Supply Chain Applications

| Area | Example |
|---|---|
| **Customer complaints** | 20% of complaint reasons = 80% of total complaints |
| **Defects** | 20% of production lines = 80% of total defects |
| **Revenue** | 20% of SKUs = 80% of revenue (→ links to ABC analysis) |
| **Suppliers** | 20% of suppliers = 80% of spend |
| **Routes** | 20% of routes = 80% of freight cost |

## Pareto vs ABC Analysis

| | Pareto | ABC Analysis |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Find root causes / prioritise problems | Classify inventory by value |
| **Principle** | 80/20 | 80-15-5 across A-B-C |
| **Output** | Ranked cause list | Item classification |
| **Application** | Quality, complaints, defects | Inventory management |
