## Supply Chain Models — CA-M-OSA (Kuldeepak Singh)
| Model | Trigger | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Channel Assembly | Assembled at 3PL, then distributed | Tata Salt, Ingram Micro |
| Make-to-Availability (MTA) | Continuous replenishment to buffer | ABB, Titan, Tata Steel |
| Make-to-Order (MTO) | Built only after customer order | Dell Computers |
| Make-to-Stock (MTS) | Based on ERP forecast | P&G, HUL, Godrej |

## 8 Wastes — TIMWOODS / DOWNTIME
| Waste                        | Description                          |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Transportation               | Unnecessary movement of goods        |
| Inventory                    | Excess stock, WIP, obsolescence cost |
| Motion                       | Unnecessary movement of people       |
| Waiting                      | Idle time, machine downtime          |
| Overproduction               | Producing beyond customer demand     |
| Overprocessing               | More work/features than needed       |
| Defects                      | Rework, scrap, quality failures      |
| Skills (Non-utilized talent) | Wrong person for the task            |

## 6 Supply Chain Drivers (Chopra)
- **Logistical:** Facilities · Inventory · Transportation
- **Cross-Functional:** Information · Sourcing · Pricing
- Each driver involves a responsiveness ↔ efficiency tradeoff

## Decision Phases
| Phase | Horizon | Decisions |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic / Design | 3–10 years | Network, location, capacity, outsourcing |
| Planning | Quarter–Year | Markets, subcontracting, inventory policy |
| Operational | Daily–Weekly | Individual orders, scheduling, picking |

## Replenishment Systems Comparison
| Factor | Q System (Fixed Qty) | P System (Fixed Period) |
|---|---|---|
| Order Trigger | Stock hits reorder point | Fixed time interval |
| Order Quantity | Fixed each time | Varies each time |
| Safety Stock | Less needed | More needed |
| Count Method | Perpetual | Periodic |

## Forecasting Methods
**Qualitative:** Delphi Technique · Market Research · Sales Force Opinion
**Quantitative:** Trend Projection · Regression · Barometric · Moving Average · Exponential Smoothing

## ABC Analysis

| A — Critical | 20% | 80% | Tight, frequent review |
| B — Medium | 30% | 15% | Moderate review |

## Key Metrics — PCS + TGT (Kuldeepak Singh)
| Group | Metrics |
|---|---|
| **PCS** (Quality) | **P**erfect Order · **C**2C Cycle · **S**upply Chain Cycle Time |
| **TGT** (Cost) | **T**urn-Earn Index · **G**MROI · **T**otal SC Cost |

## Strategic Fit Steps (Chopra)
1. Understand customer and supply chain uncertainty
2. Understand supply chain capabilities (efficiency vs responsiveness spectrum)
3. Match capabilities to customer needs — restructure if mismatch exists

## Transport Mode Priority Matrix (Kuldeepak Singh)
Priority → Factor: Speed=Air, Cost=Rail/Road, Reach=Road
Use weighted priority to select final mode or mix.
