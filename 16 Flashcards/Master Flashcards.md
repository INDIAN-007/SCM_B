## Foundations

**Q: Supply Chain Surplus formula?**
A: Customer Value − Supply Chain Cost

**Q: Three flows in a supply chain?**
A: Product flow (downstream), Information flow (both ways), Fund flow (upstream)

**Q: Three Decision Phases?**
A: Strategic (3-10yr), Planning (quarter-year), Operational (daily-weekly)

**Q: What does strategic fit mean?**
A: Alignment between competitive strategy and supply chain strategy — same customer priorities and same SC capabilities

**Q: CA-M-OSA stands for?**
A: Channel Assembly, Make-to-Order, Make-to-Stock, Make-to-Availability

**Q: Three flows a supply chain must structure?**
A: Product, Information, Funds

## Inventory

**Q: EOQ formula?**
A: √(2DS/H) where D=annual demand, S=ordering cost, H=holding cost per unit

**Q: Safety Stock formula (simple)?**
A: Z × σ_demand × √Lead Time

**Q: Z value for 95% service level?**
A: 1.65

**Q: Reorder Point formula?**
A: (Avg demand × Lead time) + Safety Stock

**Q: ABC class A: % items and % revenue?**
A: 20% of items → 80% of revenue

**Q: VED analysis measures?**
A: Criticality of spare parts: Vital, Essential, Desirable

**Q: Q system vs P system — what's the key difference?**
A: Q = fixed order quantity triggered by stock level; P = variable quantity at fixed time intervals

**Q: FIFO means?**
A: First In, First Out — oldest stock dispatched first

**Q: FEFO means?**
A: First Expired, First Out — batch with earliest expiry dispatched first

## Logistics

**Q: Cheapest mode of transport?**
A: Water/Ocean shipping (very low cost per tonne-km)

**Q: Fastest mode of transport?**
A: Air

**Q: Most flexible mode for last mile?**
A: Road

**Q: Transit time vs Lead time?**
A: Transit time = travel from origin to destination; Lead time = planning to serve (transit is a subset)

**Q: SHABD stands for?**
A: Single Hour Arrangement Based on Demand — measures logistics responsiveness (Kuldeepak)

**Q: MARGS disruption factors?**
A: Mass Customisation, Amazon Effect, Rise of Machines, Green Energy, Startups

**Q: 3PL vs 4PL?**
A: 3PL executes logistics; 4PL manages entire SC including 3PLs

## Warehousing

**Q: Standard warehouse flow?**
A: Receiving → Putaway → Storage → Picking → Packing → Dispatch

**Q: Three warehouse layout types?**
A: I-Flow, U-Flow, L-Flow

**Q: Cross-docking means?**
A: Transfer of product without storage — in-dock to out-dock, no holding

**Q: NEAD-R stands for?**
A: Need Assessment, Evaluate, Analyse, Decide, Review (warehouse selection)

**Q: What is putaway accuracy?**
A: % of items placed in correct bin/location

## Lean and Six Sigma

**Q: TIMWOODS 8 wastes?**
A: Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Overprocessing, Defects, Skills

**Q: DMAIC steps?**
A: Define, Measure, Analyse, Improve, Control

**Q: RPN formula in FMEA?**
A: Occurrence × Severity × Detection (each 1–10)

**Q: 6σ = how many DPMO?**
A: 3.4 DPMO

**Q: Kaizen means?**
A: Kai (change) + Zen (good) = continuous improvement

**Q: 5S steps?**
A: Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardise, Sustain

**Q: Poka-Yoke three strategies?**
A: Shut-Out (prevent error), Control (stop if error), Attention (warn before error)

**Q: Takt Time formula?**
A: Available production time / Customer demand per period

**Q: Gemba means?**
A: "Actual place" — go to the point of action (shop floor)

## KPIs

**Q: C2C formula?**
A: DIO + DSO − DPO (Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding)

**Q: GMROI formula?**
A: Gross Profit / Average Inventory Cost × 100

**Q: Turn-Earn Index formula?**
A: Inventory Turns × Gross Profit % × 100

**Q: Perfect Order Rate formula?**
A: (Total Orders − Erratic Orders) / Total Orders × 100

**Q: PCS in Kuldeepak's KPI framework?**
A: Perfect order, C2C cycle, Supply chain cycle time (Quality indicators)

**Q: TGT in Kuldeepak's KPI framework?**
A: Turn-Earn Index, GMROI, Total SC Cost (Cost indicators)

## Case Studies Quick Recall

**Q: Which company pioneered cross-docking?**
A: Walmart

**Q: Dell's original SC model?**
A: Build-to-Order (MTO) — direct sales, postponed final assembly, no distributors

**Q: Why did Webvan fail?**
A: Higher transport + labour cost than supermarkets; insufficient delivery density

**Q: Why did Borders fail?**
A: Failed to adapt SC to digital/online; outsourced online operations to Amazon

**Q: What made Seven-Eleven Japan successful?**
A: High-density stores + very frequent replenishment + real-time demand-based product mix

**Q: Amazon's C2C in 2013?**
A: −10.53 weeks (collected from customers 10+ weeks before paying suppliers)
