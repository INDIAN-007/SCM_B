# Pricing Driver

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.3 & Ch.16
> **Tags:** #drivers #pricing #revenue

---

## Role in Supply Chain

Pricing determines how much a firm charges for its product or service. It influences customer demand patterns — which in turn affects the entire SC.

---

## Pricing Strategies

### EDLP — Everyday Low Pricing (Walmart)
- Stable, predictable demand
- Entire SC can focus on consistent efficiency
- No promotional demand spikes
- Lower holding cost, smoother operations

### Promotional Pricing
- Creates demand spikes
- Forward buying by retailers (buy more than needed at low price)
- Amplifies bullwhip effect
- Higher inventory cost, lower efficiency

---

## Revenue Management Techniques (Chopra Ch.16)

| Technique | When | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Differential pricing** | Multiple customer segments | Business vs economy class |
| **Dynamic pricing** | Perishable assets with time value | Hotel rooms, airline seats |
| **Peak pricing** | Seasonal demand | Ski resorts, festive shipping |
| **Bulk contracts + spot** | Commodity markets | Oil, freight capacity |

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Profit margin | Revenue minus COGS and expenses |
| Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) | How quickly customers pay |
| Revenue per customer | Average transaction value |
| Return rate | % of products returned |
| Margin by SKU / segment | Which products / channels are most profitable |

---

## Pricing's Effect on Supply Chain

- **High price + low volume** → small, specialised SC needed
- **Low price + high volume** → efficient, high-throughput SC needed
- Differential pricing can attract both types of customers simultaneously — SC structured to serve both
