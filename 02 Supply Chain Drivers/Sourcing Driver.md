# Sourcing Driver

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.3 & Ch.15
> **Tags:** #drivers #sourcing #procurement

---

## Role in Supply Chain

Sourcing decisions determine **who performs which supply chain activity**. The goal is to assign activities to parties that can increase supply chain surplus.

---

## Key Sourcing Decisions

### In-House vs Outsource
- **Outsource:** Lower cost, access to expertise, but less control
- **In-house:** More control, IP protection, but higher fixed cost

### Supplier Selection Criteria
- Cost (total cost of ownership, not just price)
- Quality reliability
- Lead time and responsiveness
- Flexibility (ability to handle volume changes)
- Financial stability
- Geographic location (risk diversification)

### Supplier Base Strategy
- **Single source:** Lowest cost, maximum leverage, high supply risk
- **Dual source:** Balance of cost and risk
- **Multiple source:** Highest resilience, harder to manage

---

## Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

TCO goes beyond purchase price:
```
TCO = Purchase Price
    + Inbound Transportation
    + Receiving & Inspection
    + Inventory Holding (affected by lead time)
    + Rework / Defect Cost
    + Risk Premium (supply disruption)
    + Relationship management cost
```

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) | COGS / (Accounts Payable × 365) — measures payment terms |
| Procurement cost as % of COGS | Efficiency of purchasing function |
| Supplier lead time | Average time from PO to delivery |
| Supplier defect rate | Quality metric |
| On-time delivery by supplier | Service level metric |
| Fraction of on-time / in-full deliveries | OTIF measure |

## 3PL vs 4PL (from Kuldeepak)

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| 3PL (Third Party Logistics) | Outsourced logistics execution (transport, warehouse) |
| 4PL (Fourth Party Logistics) | Full SC management outsourced — 4PL manages 3PLs |
