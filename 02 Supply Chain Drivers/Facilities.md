# Facilities Driver

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.3
> **Tags:** #drivers #facilities #network

---

## Role in Supply Chain

Facilities are the **physical locations** where product is stored, assembled, or fabricated. They are a key driver of SC responsiveness and efficiency.

---

## Types of Facilities

| Type | Function |
|---|---|
| **Production** | Transform inputs → outputs (raw → WIP → finished goods) |
| **Storage (Warehouse / DC)** | Hold inventory; may perform value-added activities |
| **Cross-docking** | Transfer product without storage — sorter / transfer point |
| **Retail stores** | Customer-facing locations holding stock |

---

## Key Decisions

### Location Decisions
- Where to locate facilities — impacts cost, responsiveness, and risk
- **Factors:** labour cost, infrastructure, proximity to customers/suppliers, taxes, land cost
- More facilities → higher responsiveness; fewer → higher efficiency

### Capacity Decisions
- **Excess capacity** → expensive but flexible
- **Tight capacity** → efficient but inflexible during demand spikes

### Operations Methodology
- **Product focus:** Each facility makes a full product range (responsive, but lower scale)
- **Functional focus:** Each facility performs one function for all products (efficient, requires coordination)

---

## Key Metrics for Facilities

| Metric | Formula / Description |
|---|---|
| Capacity utilisation | Actual output / Design capacity |
| Processing / setup / down / idle time | Proportion of total time |
| Production cost per unit | Total production cost / Units produced |
| Theoretical flow / cycle time | Time through value chain without waiting |
| Actual average flow time | Includes waiting and queues |
| Flow time efficiency | Theoretical / Actual flow time |
| Product variety | SKUs produced at each facility |
| Volume contribution | Revenue per facility |

---

## Network Design Principle

The number and location of facilities is one of the **most expensive and difficult** SC decisions to reverse. Mistakes cost years of sub-optimal performance.

**Centralisation** → Lower per-unit cost, higher safety stock, lower fill rate risk due to pooling
**Decentralisation** → More responsive, but higher total inventory and facility cost
