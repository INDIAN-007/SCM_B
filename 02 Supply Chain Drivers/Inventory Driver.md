# Inventory Driver

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.3 · Kuldeepak Ch.4
> **Tags:** #drivers #inventory

---

## Role in Supply Chain

Inventory is held throughout the supply chain to buffer mismatches between supply and demand. It is a **major cost driver** and a key lever for responsiveness.

---

## Types of Inventory

| Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Cycle Inventory** | Average inventory between orders; driven by EOQ logic |
| **Safety Inventory** | Buffer against demand and supply uncertainty |
| **Seasonal Inventory** | Built in advance of predictable demand peaks |
| **Pipeline Inventory** | In-transit inventory; driven by lead time × demand rate |

---

## Key Tradeoffs

- **Higher inventory** → more responsive, fewer stockouts, higher holding cost
- **Lower inventory** → lower holding cost, risk of stockouts, lower responsiveness

---

## Key Metrics (Chopra)

| Metric | Formula |
|---|---|
| Inventory Turns (INVT) | COGS / Average Inventory |
| Days Inventory Outstanding | 365 / Inventory Turns |
| Average inventory | (Opening + Closing) / 2 |
| Fraction satisfied from stock | Fill rate |
| Stockout frequency | No. of stockouts / Review periods |
| Carrying (holding) cost | % of inventory value per year |

---

## Financial Impact

- **Amazon 2013:** INVT = 7.31 turns
- **Nordstrom 2013:** INVT = 5.46 turns (impressive for a multi-store retailer)
- High turns = lower average inventory, less holding cost, faster cash cycle

---

## Inventory Costs (Kuldeepak)

| Cost Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Holding cost | Rent, utilities, insurance, obsolescence, capital tied up |
| Ordering cost | Purchase processing, receiving, inspection per order |
| Shortage cost | Lost sales, emergency procurement, customer dissatisfaction |
| Obsolescence cost | Expired, damaged, or unsaleable stock |
