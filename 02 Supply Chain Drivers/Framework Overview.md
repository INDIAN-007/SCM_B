## The Six Drivers

Strategic fit requires balancing **responsiveness** and **efficiency**. Six drivers determine where a supply chain sits on this spectrum:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           SUPPLY CHAIN PERFORMANCE               │
├─────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│   LOGISTICAL        │   CROSS-FUNCTIONAL         │
│   Facilities        │   Information              │
│   Inventory         │   Sourcing                 │
│   Transportation    │   Pricing                  │
└─────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Responsiveness vs Efficiency Tradeoffs

| Driver | More Responsive (↑) | More Efficient (↑) |
|---|---|---|
| **Facilities** | More locations, decentralised | Fewer, centralised, higher utilisation |
| **Inventory** | Higher stock levels | Lower stock levels |
| **Transportation** | Faster modes (air, express) | Slower, consolidated modes (rail, ocean) |
| **Information** | More investment, real-time data | Less investment, batch data |
| **Sourcing** | Responsive, flexible suppliers | Low-cost suppliers |
| **Pricing** | Premium pricing for fast delivery | EDLP / bulk pricing for efficiency |

---

## Drivers Do Not Act Independently

- More facilities → more inventory required
- Higher inventory → more responsive but less efficient
- Faster transport → less inventory needed but higher cost
- Better information → improves both responsiveness and efficiency

---

## Walmart Example — All 6 Drivers Aligned to Efficiency + Responsiveness

| Driver | Walmart's Approach |
|---|---|
| Inventory | Cross-docking — no warehouse holding; only store stock |
| Transportation | Owned fleet — higher cost but improves product availability |
| Facilities | Centralised DCs serving clusters of stores |
| Information | RFID, EDI, shared supplier data — real-time visibility |
| Sourcing | Large orders to efficient suppliers → economies of scale |
| Pricing | EDLP — stable demand, no promotional spikes |

---

## IKEA Example — Low-Cost Efficiency Focus

- Modular, unassembled furniture → components in inventory (cheaper than finished goods)
- Large, stable replenishment orders → suppliers focus on efficiency
- Dense packing → low-cost transport modes
- Large stores → high availability with low inventory per SKU
