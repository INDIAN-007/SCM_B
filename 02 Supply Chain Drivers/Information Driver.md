## Role in Supply Chain

Information is **the glue that holds supply chains together**. It enables coordination across all stages and is the foundation for every SC decision.

---

## What Good Information Enables

- **Coordination:** Each stage acts based on shared, accurate data
- **Optimization:** Decisions are made on facts, not guesses
- **Visibility:** Real-time tracking of inventory, shipments, demand
- **Risk reduction:** Early warning of disruptions

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Forecast horizon | How far ahead can you forecast reliably? |
| Frequency of update | How often are forecasts revised? |
| Forecast error | MAD, MAPE — accuracy of predictions |
| Bullwhip factor | Ratio of SC order variability to customer demand variability |
| Order fill rates by stage | Information quality affects fill rates |

---

## Information Technology Tools in Supply Chains

| Tool | Function |
|---|---|
| ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) | Central data platform — finance, procurement, production, logistics |
| WMS (Warehouse Management System) | Manages warehouse operations — receiving, storage, picking, shipping |
| TMS (Transportation Management System) | Route planning, carrier selection, freight audit |
| CRM (Customer Relationship Management) | Customer order history, preferences, demand signals |
| EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) | Structured data exchange between trading partners |
| RFID / Barcode | Real-time product tracking at item/case/pallet level |
| Control Tower | Centralised visibility and analytics platform |

---

## Walmart's Information Advantage

Walmart invested heavily in IT before competitors — sharing POS data with suppliers in real-time allowed suppliers to replenish exactly what was selling. This is a major source of their efficiency advantage.
