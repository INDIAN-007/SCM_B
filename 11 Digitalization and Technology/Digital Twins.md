## Definition

A Digital Twin is "an evolving digital profile of the historical and current behaviour of a physical object or process that helps to optimize business performance." — Deloitte University Press

It is more capable than CAD or basic IoT — it **monitors, collects, stores, and analyses** data from physical systems to generate actionable insights.

---

## Digital Twin vs IoT vs CAD

| Technology | Capability |
|---|---|
| **CAD** | 3D design model — static representation |
| **IoT** | Sensors collect real-time data — reactive |
| **Digital Twin** | Full simulation + historical + real-time + AI analytics — predictive and prescriptive |

---

## Digital Thread (Related Concept — Kuldeepak)

Originated in military aircraft industry; now used in industrial manufacturing.

**Digital Thread:** A common link (process, activity, or attribute) that connects **digitally across the complete product lifecycle** — from design → development → manufacturing → quality check.

It enables creation of a digital twin that optimises business performance through near-real-time visibility and analysis.

## Supply Chain Applications

| Application | Value |
|---|---|
| **Warehouse simulation** | Test layout changes before physical implementation |
| **Supply chain network modelling** | Simulate impact of adding/removing facilities |
| **Predictive maintenance** | Predict equipment failure before it occurs |
| **Risk simulation** | Model disruption scenarios and response options |
| **Demand simulation** | Test how SC responds to demand spikes |
| **Sustainability modelling** | Simulate carbon impact of routing decisions |
