> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.5 · Chopra Ch.A (IT Chapter)

## Role of AI/ML in Supply Chain

AI and ML transform supply chains from reactive to **predictive and prescriptive** — moving from "what happened?" to "what will happen?" and "what should we do?"

---

## Key Applications

| Area | AI/ML Application |
|---|---|
| **Demand Forecasting** | ML models outperform traditional time series by capturing complex patterns (seasonality, promotions, weather, social trends) |
| **Inventory Optimisation** | Determine optimal safety stock and reorder points dynamically per SKU per location |
| **Route Optimisation** | Real-time route planning considering traffic, weather, delivery windows (Google Maps, Uber Freight) |
| **Supplier Risk** | Predict supplier financial distress or quality failures before they occur |
| **Anomaly Detection** | Flag unusual demand patterns, fraud, data entry errors |
| **Natural Language Processing** | Extract insights from contracts, emails, supplier communications |
| **Computer Vision** | Automated quality inspection, damage detection, shelf audit |
| **Chatbots / Virtual Assistants** | Supplier and customer query handling |
| **Prescriptive Analytics** | Recommend optimal SC decisions in real time |

---

## AI in Control Towers (Kuldeepak)

Prescriptive analysis — AI not only predicts events likely to occur but **recommends actions to mitigate risk**.

Three levels:
1. Descriptive → What happened?
2. Predictive → What will happen?
3. Prescriptive → What should we do? (AI-powered)

---

## Chopra's IT Framework (Ch.A)

IT in supply chains has evolved through stages:
1. **EDI** — structured data exchange between trading partners
2. **ERP** — integrated internal data (SAP, Oracle)
3. **SCM Suite** — planning tools (APS, demand planning, TMS, WMS)
4. **Internet-era** — visibility platforms, marketplaces
5. **Cloud + AI era** — real-time analytics, ML forecasting, autonomous decision-making
