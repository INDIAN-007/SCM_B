# IT Framework for Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.A
> **Tags:** #technology #it #erp #systems

---

## Role of IT in Supply Chain

IT enables the **sharing of information** across all stages of the supply chain — the foundation of coordination, planning, and optimisation.

Without IT:
- Each stage operates with limited visibility
- Bullwhip effect is amplified
- Planning is reactive, not proactive

---

## Chopra's Supply Chain IT Framework

### Three Macro Process IT Systems

| Macro Process | IT Systems |
|---|---|
| **SRM** (Supplier Relationship) | Supplier portals, procurement systems (Ariba, Coupa), supply collaboration |
| **ISCM** (Internal SC Management) | ERP (SAP, Oracle), APS, WMS, TMS, MES, demand planning |
| **CRM** (Customer Relationship) | CRM systems, e-commerce platforms, order management |

---

## Key Supply Chain IT Systems

| System | Function |
|---|---|
| **ERP** | Central data backbone — finance, HR, procurement, production, logistics |
| **WMS** (Warehouse Management) | Receiving, putaway, picking, shipping, inventory accuracy |
| **TMS** (Transportation Management) | Route planning, carrier selection, freight audit, tracking |
| **APS** (Advanced Planning & Scheduling) | Demand planning, supply planning, S&OP |
| **MES** (Manufacturing Execution) | Shop floor control, production tracking |
| **EDI** | Structured electronic data interchange between trading partners |
| **RFID / IoT** | Real-time product tracking at item/pallet level |
| **BI / Analytics** | Reporting, dashboards, predictive analytics |
| **Control Tower** | End-to-end visibility and exception management |

## The Future of IT in Supply Chain (Chopra)

- **Cloud-based platforms** — eliminate on-premise infrastructure; enable rapid scaling
- **API-driven ecosystems** — real-time integration between systems and partners
- **AI/ML** — autonomous demand forecasting, pricing, replenishment
- **Blockchain** — immutable traceability and smart contracts
- **Digital twins** — simulation of entire SC networks
- **Autonomous supply chains** — self-correcting systems with minimal human intervention

---

## IT Risk Management (Chopra)

Key risks to SC IT:
- Cyber attacks (ransomware on logistics systems — major disruption)
- System downtime (if ERP goes down, fulfilment stops)
- Data integrity issues (garbage in → garbage out for planning)
- Integration failures between systems

Mitigations:
- Backup systems and disaster recovery plans
- Cybersecurity protocols (zero trust architecture)
- Data governance and master data management
