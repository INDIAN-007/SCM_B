# Green Logistics

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.5 · Chopra Ch.17
> **Tags:** #sustainability #greenlogistics #environment

---

## Definition

Green logistics aims to reduce the environmental impact of logistics activities — particularly carbon emissions, noise, and congestion — while maintaining or improving service levels and cost efficiency.

---

## Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fleet electrification** | Replace diesel trucks with electric or hydrogen vehicles |
| **Route optimisation** | Fewer km = less fuel, less emissions (TMS + AI routing) |
| **Load consolidation** | Full truckload is more efficient per unit than LTL |
| **Modal shift** | Rail and water have lower CO2 per tonne-km vs road |
| **Green packaging** | Reduce packaging, use recycled materials, eliminate single-use plastics |
| **Reverse logistics** | Efficient returns reduce wasted transport |
| **Green warehousing** | Solar panels, LED, rainwater harvesting, LEED certification |
| **Local sourcing** | Shorter supply chains reduce transport emissions |
| **Collaborative logistics** | Share transport capacity with other companies (co-opetition) |

---

## Carbon Accounting

| Scope | Covers |
|---|---|
| **Scope 1** | Direct emissions from owned operations (owned fleet, on-site energy) |
| **Scope 2** | Indirect emissions from purchased electricity/heat |
| **Scope 3** | All other upstream/downstream emissions — supplier manufacturing, customer use, logistics, end-of-life |

For most SC-intensive businesses, **Scope 3** is 70–90% of total emissions — and the hardest to measure and reduce.
