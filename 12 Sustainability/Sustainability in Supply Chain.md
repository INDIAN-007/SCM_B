# Sustainability in Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.17 · Kuldeepak Ch.5
> **Tags:** #sustainability #esg #green

---

## Definition

Sustainability in supply chain involves managing **environmental, social, and economic** impacts of SC activities — balancing profitability with responsibility to people and the planet.

---

## Three Pillars of Corporate Social Responsibility (Chopra Ch.17)

| Pillar | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Environmental** | Carbon emissions, waste, water use, biodiversity, packaging |
| **Social** | Labour practices, human rights, community impact, diversity |
| **Economic** | Fair wages, responsible sourcing, anti-corruption |

---

## Sustainability Drivers in Supply Chain

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|
| Customer pressure | Consumers increasingly prefer sustainable brands |
| Regulatory compliance | Carbon taxes, ESG disclosure requirements, import bans on unsustainable goods |
| Investor pressure | ESG scores affect access to capital |
| Cost reduction | Green logistics often cheaper long-term (electric vehicles, route optimisation) |
| Risk management | Climate change disrupts SC infrastructure; preparedness is resilience |

---

## Key Environmental Actions in SC

| Action | Impact |
|---|---|
| Route optimisation | Fewer km driven = lower fuel use and emissions |
| Modal shift (road → rail/sea) | Lower carbon per tonne-km |
| Electric / hydrogen vehicles | Zero tailpipe emissions |
| Packaging reduction / recycled materials | Less plastic, lower weight |
| Reverse logistics | Product recovery, recycling, refurbishment |
| Supplier sustainability standards | Extend sustainability upstream |
| Renewable energy in warehouses | Solar panels, LED lighting |

---

## Kuldeepak's Green Energy Context

- Fossil fuels are finite; DHL sustainability report warns of serious availability issues by 2040
- Alternate fuels: Solar, Electricity, Hydrogen Fuel Cells, Biomass — in development
- Already lower in cost and CO2 compared to diesel in many markets
- Strategic SC leaders should begin gradual deployment now

---

## Tragedy of the Commons (Chopra)

When a shared resource (clean air, ocean fisheries, fresh water) is unmanaged:
- Individual actors pursue their self-interest
- Resource is depleted beyond recovery
- Everyone suffers

**SC Implication:** Without regulation or incentive design, firms will externalise environmental costs (pollute, overfish) at the expense of the common good. Government regulation and industry self-governance are essential.

## Sustainability Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Carbon footprint (Scope 1, 2, 3) | Direct, indirect, and value chain emissions |
| Packaging waste % | Recyclable vs total packaging |
| Water usage | Litres per unit produced |
| Supplier audit score | ESG compliance of Tier 1/2/3 suppliers |
| % renewable energy | Share of operations on renewable power |
| Return / recycling rate | % products recovered at end of life |
