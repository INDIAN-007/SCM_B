# Apparel & Fashion — SCM Overview

> **Tags:** #industry #apparel #fashion #retail #SCM

---

## Industry Profile

| Attribute | Characteristic |
|---|---|
| **Demand Pattern** | Seasonal (2–4 seasons), trend-driven, unpredictable |
| **Product Life** | Short (fast fashion: 2–4 weeks); Moderate (basics: 6–12 months) |
| **Key Risk** | Overstocking = markdowns; Understocking = lost revenue + trend missed |
| **Lead Times** | Asia sourcing: 90–120 days; Nearshore: 30–60 days; Local: 2–4 weeks |
| **Returns** | 20–40% for online fashion |
| **Companies** | Zara, H&M, Uniqlo, Myntra, Shein, Cotton On, Marks & Spencer |

---

## Core Methods

### 1. Fast Fashion SCM (Zara Model)
- Design → Manufacture → Store in **3–5 weeks** (vs industry average 6 months)
- Small batch production → test demand → scale winners → abandon losers
- Near-shoring: Manufacture in Spain/Morocco/Turkey for Europe proximity
- **Result:** 500–1000 new styles per week; items sell at full price; minimal markdown

### 2. Open-to-Buy (OTB) Planning
- Buying budget allocated by category, season, and month
- `OTB = Planned Sales + Planned EOM Stock − Planned BOM Stock − Planned Receipts`
- Controls how much inventory buyers can commit to; prevents over-buying

### 3. Markdown Management
- At end of season, mark down unsold items progressively
- **Markdown cadence:** -20% → -30% → -50% → clearance
- **Tip:** Markdown early — smaller discount, more stock sold; late markdown = deep cut, less recovery

### 4. Postponement for Apparel
- Produce undyed/unprinted fabric → dye/print closer to season based on demand signal
- **Example:** H&M produces grey fabric; decides colour mix 4 weeks before delivery based on trend data

### 5. Size Curve Analysis
- Demand per size is not equal: S/M/L/XL have different demand ratios per region, channel, and demographic
- Wrong size curve = stockout in M, dead stock in XXL
- **Approach:** Analyse historical sell-through by size → apply size curve to every buy

## Apparel KPIs

| KPI | Target |
|---|---|
| Sell-Through % | > 75% at full price; > 90% total |
| Markdown % of Revenue | < 15% |
| Weeks of Cover (WOC) | 6–10 weeks in season |
| OTB Accuracy | ±10% of plan |
| Returns Rate | < 20% (online fashion) |
| Time to Market (design to shelf) | < 8 weeks (fast fashion) |

---

## Tips

1. **Design and buying must work with supply chain** — a 120-day lead time supplier cannot support a 4-week trend
2. **Test-and-repeat model** — buy small, sell fast, repeat winners; eliminates demand uncertainty
3. **Size ratio is as important as total quantity** — buying the wrong size ratio kills sell-through
4. **Returns data = buying intelligence** — analyse why items are returned (wrong size, quality, not as described) to improve future buys
5. **Near-source for trend items; offshore for basics** — basics have predictable demand; near-source for reactive, trend-driven styles
