# Capacity Planning

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.8 · Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #planning #capacity #production

---

## Definition

Capacity planning determines the **production, storage, and transportation capacity** needed to meet customer demand — now and in the future.

---

## Types of Capacity

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Design capacity** | Maximum possible output under ideal conditions |
| **Effective capacity** | Expected output allowing for normal downtime, maintenance |
| **Actual output** | Real output achieved |

```
Capacity Utilisation = Actual Output / Design Capacity
Efficiency          = Actual Output / Effective Capacity
```

---

## Capacity Planning Horizons

| Horizon | Actions |
|---|---|
| **Long-term (strategic)** | Build/close facilities, major equipment investment |
| **Medium-term (tactical)** | Hire workforce, lease equipment, arrange subcontracting |
| **Short-term (operational)** | Overtime, shift changes, temporary workers |

---

## Capacity and Responsiveness Tradeoff

- **Excess capacity** → expensive to maintain, but enables fast response to demand spikes
- **Tight capacity** → efficient (high utilisation), but risks stockouts during peaks

**Safety capacity** = excess capacity held strategically = analogous to safety stock for inventory

---

## Capacity in Supply Chain Network

- More plants → more responsive but lower utilisation per plant
- Fewer, larger plants → higher efficiency but less responsive to regional demand
