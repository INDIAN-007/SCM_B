# Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP)

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.9
> **Tags:** #planning #sop #coordination

---

## Definition

S&OP is a **cross-functional planning process** that aligns sales (demand) plans with operations (supply) plans at the executive level, over a monthly rolling horizon.

It bridges the gap between strategy and execution.

---

## Purpose

- Align marketing, sales, operations, finance around a single demand and supply plan
- Balance customer service vs inventory cost vs capacity utilisation
- Manage predictable variability in demand (seasonality, promotions)
- Single version of truth across departments

---

## S&OP Process — Monthly Cycle

```
Step 1: Data Gathering → collect historical sales, inventory levels, capacity status
Step 2: Demand Review → statistical forecast + marketing / sales input
Step 3: Supply Review → capacity review, supplier capacity, constraints
Step 4: Pre-S&OP Meeting → resolve gaps between supply and demand plans
Step 5: Executive S&OP → final decisions on priorities, investments, trade-offs
Step 6: Execute → release to MPS, procurement, logistics
```

---

## Responding to Predictable Variability (Chopra Ch.9)

When demand is seasonal but predictable, firms can:

| Option | Description | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| **Build inventory** | Produce at level rate, accumulate stock during off-peak | Holding cost |
| **Vary production rate** | Chase strategy — hire/use overtime in peak | Labour cost |
| **Use flexible capacity** | Subcontract or use temporary labour | Higher per-unit cost |
| **Vary price** | Promotions in off-peak, premium in peak | Demand management |
| **Offer back orders** | Accept orders now, deliver later | Customer experience risk |
| **Reduce lead time** | Build and deliver faster during peak | Responsiveness investment |

---

## Red Tomato Tools Example (Chopra Ch.9)

A seasonality problem solved through S&OP:
- Peak demand in April; low demand in November–February
- Optimal solution: mix of inventory build-up + some overtime in peak months
- Solved as LP (Linear Programming) to minimise total cost
