> **Tags:** #network #risk #resilience

## The Resilience Framework (Kuldeepak)

Three questions every SC leader must ask:
1. Is a disruption strategy in place?
2. How quickly can the SC be restored after disruption?
3. Are teams trained, prepared, and empowered to handle change?

**Three pillars:** Visibility · Flexibility · Agility

---

## Risk Identification Framework

| Category | Risk Examples |
|---|---|
| **Supply side** | Supplier bankruptcy, quality failure, single-source dependency |
| **Demand side** | Sudden demand collapse, demand spike beyond capacity |
| **Logistics** | Port congestion, carrier bankruptcy, fuel price spike |
| **Geopolitical** | Trade war tariffs, sanctions, border closures |
| **Natural** | Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes affecting plants or ports |
| **Pandemic / Health** | Labour unavailability, demand pattern disruption |
| **Technology** | Cyber attack on supply chain systems, IT failure |
| **Financial** | Currency devaluation, supplier financial distress |

---

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Diversification** | Multiple suppliers, multiple plants, multiple logistics routes |
| **Redundancy** | Buffer inventory, backup capacity |
| **Flexibility** | Contracts allowing volume ramp up/down; multi-capable plants |
| **Visibility** | Real-time tracking of inventory, shipments, supplier capacity |
| **Collaboration** | Joint risk planning with key suppliers |
| **Insurance** | Business interruption, cargo, political risk insurance |
| **Contracts** | Force majeure clauses, penalty and reward structures |

## FMEA for Supply Chain Risk (Kuldeepak)

Apply Failure Mode and Effects Analysis:
```
RPN = Occurrence × Severity × Detection
```
Focus improvement efforts on highest RPN risks first.

---

## Tragedy of the Commons (Chopra Ch.17)

A concept applied to supply chain sustainability risk:
- When shared resources are unregulated, individual actors deplete them (overfishing, CO2 emissions)
- SC managers must account for these externalities
- Regulation and incentive design are critical
