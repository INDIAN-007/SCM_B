# Omni-Channel Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.4
> **Tags:** #network #omnichannel #ecommerce #retail

---

## Definition

An omni-channel supply chain serves customers seamlessly across **all channels** — physical store, website, mobile, phone — with consistent product availability, pricing, and experience.

---

## Key Characteristics

- Single inventory pool visible across channels
- Customer can order through any channel and return through any
- Fulfilment can happen from store, DC, or supplier depending on availability
- Real-time inventory visibility is essential

---

## Fulfilment Options in Omni-Channel

| Option | Description |
|---|---|
| **Ship from DC** | Standard e-commerce model; centralised fulfilment |
| **Ship from Store** | Use retail store inventory for online orders; reduces shipping distance |
| **BOPIS (Click & Collect)** | Buy online, pick up in store; high customer convenience |
| **BORIS (Buy Online, Return In Store)** | Returns handled at physical store; improves customer trust |
| **Same-day delivery** | Micro-fulfilment centres near customers for 2–4 hour delivery |

---

## Challenges

| Challenge | Impact |
|---|---|
| Inventory fragmentation | Same SKU in multiple channels — coordination is complex |
| Return management | Online has 3–5x higher return rates than physical retail |
| Last-mile cost | Delivering individual packages is far more expensive than store replenishment |
| Channel conflict | Store staff may resist fulfilment for online orders |
| IT integration | WMS + OMS + ERP must be tightly integrated |

---

## Walmart vs Amazon Contrast (Chopra)

- Walmart built a dominant brick-and-mortar supply chain — efficient, cross-docked, high inventory turns at store level
- This same SC is **not optimal** for small-format stores or online
- Amazon built for online first — large FCs, algorithmic picking, owned delivery
- Both are now converging toward omni-channel by acquiring/building capabilities in the other's domain
