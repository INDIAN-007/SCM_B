# Borders — Supply Chain Failure Case

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1
> **Tags:** #casestudy #failure #retail #adaptation

---

## Background

Borders was one of the largest US bookstore chains in the 1990s-2000s. It declared bankruptcy in 2010 — a textbook case of failing to adapt SC to a changing environment.

---

## The Rise (1990s–2004)

- Along with Barnes & Noble, dominated book/music retailing through superstore concept
- Superstores offered 100,000 titles vs <10,000 at local bookstores
- Higher inventory turns than local stores
- Lower operating costs per dollar of sales
- 2004 peak: ~$4B revenue, $132M profit

**SC advantage:** Aggregation in large superstores enabled variety + efficiency.

---

## The Decline (2004–2010)

### What Changed
| Threat | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Amazon** | Far greater variety (millions of titles) at lower cost via online + distribution centres |
| **Digital books (Kindle, Nook)** | Physical book demand collapsed |
| **Music streaming/downloads** | CD sales collapsed |
| **Online competition** | Price comparison eliminated Borders' pricing power |

### Borders' SC Failure

Borders outsourced its online sales to Amazon (ironically) — meaning it had no digital SC capability when it needed one most.

When it tried to build its own online presence, it was years behind Amazon's infrastructure and customer base.

### The Critical Lesson (Chopra)

> "A failure to adapt supply chains to a changing environment can significantly hurt performance."

Unlike Dell, which successfully adapted its SC from responsiveness to efficiency, Borders failed to build the SC capabilities needed for the digital environment.

---

## Contrast: Barnes & Noble

Barnes & Noble also struggled but survived by:
- Launching the Nook e-reader (building digital SC capability)
- Maintaining physical stores for browsing/discovery (SC serving a different customer need)
- Partnership strategy for distribution

---

## Lessons

1. **SC adaptation is not optional** — markets change and SCs must change with them
2. Outsourcing core capabilities (like Borders outsourcing online to Amazon) destroys future strategic options
3. Physical retail and digital retail require fundamentally different SC structures
4. The time to build new SC capabilities is before they're urgently needed
