# Walmart — Supply Chain Case Study

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1, Ch.3
> **Tags:** #casestudy #walmart #retail #efficiency

---

## Overview

Walmart is the world's largest retailer by revenue ($482B in 2016). Its success is built on **supply chain efficiency** — the ability to deliver consistent product availability at the lowest possible cost.

---

## Key Supply Chain Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Cross-docking** | Pioneered cross-docking — products transferred at DC without storage. Only stocked at stores. |
| **Distribution centre clustering** | Stores built in clusters around DCs — efficient replenishment |
| **Owned fleet** | Runs its own truck fleet — higher cost but better control and responsiveness |
| **EDLP (Everyday Low Pricing)** | Stable demand; no promotional spikes; easier SC planning |
| **Information sharing** | Shares POS data with suppliers in real time (pioneered retailer-supplier data sharing) |
| **Vendor collaboration** | Large orders to efficient suppliers; suppliers plan production on Walmart's demand signals |
| **RFID investment** | Early adopter of RFID for inventory tracking at item level |

---

## Financial Scale

- 2016 Net Income: $14.7B on revenues of $482B
- One of the highest inventory turns in brick-and-mortar retail
- Low-cost structure achieved through SC efficiency — not just negotiating leverage

---

## SC Drivers Alignment (Chopra)

| Driver | Walmart's Approach |
|---|---|
| Facilities | Centralised DCs serving store clusters; few DCs, many stores |
| Inventory | Cross-docking → no DC inventory; only store stock |
| Transportation | Owned fleet → higher cost, better responsiveness and control |
| Information | RFID, EDI, shared POS data with suppliers |
| Sourcing | Large order volumes → economies of scale for suppliers |
| Pricing | EDLP → smooth, predictable demand across SC |

---

## Challenges

- Difficulty replicating efficiency in small-format stores
- Online channel (Walmart.com) requires different SC than large stores
- Had to acquire Jet.com and restructure SC for e-commerce competition with Amazon

---

## Lessons

1. Information sharing with suppliers creates a win-win — lower costs, better availability
2. EDLP is as much a supply chain strategy as a pricing strategy
3. The SC structure for large-format stores doesn't automatically work for online or small stores
4. Owning your own transportation can be strategically justified even if more expensive
