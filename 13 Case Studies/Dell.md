# Dell — Supply Chain Case Study

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1, Ch.2
> **Tags:** #casestudy #dell #buildtoorder #adaptation

---

## Phase 1 (1993–2006): Build-to-Order Excellence

### Strategy
- Sell directly to end customers (no distributors or retailers)
- Manufacture only after customer order arrives
- Centralise manufacturing; postpone final assembly

### SC Design
- Customer orders online/phone → order goes to factory
- Components sourced from suppliers held nearby (VMI hubs)
- Final assembly completed within hours of order
- PC shipped directly to customer (merging with monitor from Sony en route)

### Results
- Revenue grew to $56B by 2006
- Net income $3.5B
- Low component inventory (postponement to latest point)
- Wide customisation at competitive price

### Why It Worked
- Customer demand: high customisation, willing to wait 5–7 days
- Technology: stable enough that build-to-order was feasible
- Strategic fit: responsive SC matched customer priority for customisation

---

## Phase 2 (2007 onwards): Adaptation

### What Changed
- Customers shifted to wanting standard, lower-cost PCs
- Hardware capabilities standardised → less need for customisation
- Retail channel became important (customers wanted to see/buy immediately)

### SC Adaptation
- Started selling through Walmart, Best Buy (via retailers)
- Outsourced assembly to Foxconn (low-cost, build-to-stock model)
- Shifted from responsiveness focus to efficiency focus

### Lesson
Strategic fit must be continuously maintained as market evolves. Unlike Borders, Dell successfully adapted.

## Key Concepts Illustrated

| Concept | Dell Example |
|---|---|
| Strategic Fit | Build-to-order SC matched customer need for customisation (1993–2006) |
| SC Adaptation | SC evolved as customer priorities shifted to cost and standardisation |
| Postponement | Final assembly delayed until order received |
| Make-to-Order (MTO) | Classic example of MTO supply chain |
| VMI | Supplier-managed component inventory near Dell plants |
