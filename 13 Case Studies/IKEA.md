# IKEA — Supply Chain Case Study

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.3
> **Tags:** #casestudy #ikea #retail #efficiency

---

## Overview

IKEA's supply chain is built around a single insight: **furniture components are much cheaper to store and ship than assembled furniture**. This drives every SC design decision.

---

## Key SC Innovations

| Innovation | Description |
|---|---|
| **Flat-pack design** | Furniture designed to disassemble and pack flat — reduces volume 80%+ vs assembled |
| **Customer assembly** | Customer does the final assembly — eliminates a manufacturing step |
| **Component inventory (not finished goods)** | Large stores carry components, not assembled furniture — lower inventory cost |
| **Large store as SC hub** | Store = showroom + DC + pickup point — eliminates retailer and distributor |
| **Dense packing = low transport cost** | Flat pack enables maximum container density → lowest ocean/road freight cost |
| **Stable, large replenishment orders** | Enables suppliers to focus on efficiency and economies of scale |

---

## SC Drivers Alignment (Chopra)

| Driver | IKEA's Approach |
|---|---|
| Facilities | Large stores replace DCs — low number of stocking locations |
| Inventory | Component inventory is far cheaper than finished goods |
| Transportation | Dense, flat-pack enables low-cost bulk shipping |
| Sourcing | Stable, large orders → efficient, low-cost suppliers |
| Pricing | Low prices enabled by efficient SC throughout |

---

## Contrast: Custom US Furniture Makers

- High variety finished products → impossible to stock all at retail
- Retailer carries one sample → customer orders custom → production to order
- Long lead time (6–12 weeks) accepted because of high customisation
- SC structured around responsiveness for last-mile delivery

This shows how different competitive strategies require fundamentally different SC structures — and both can be successful if aligned.

---

## Lessons

1. Product design IS supply chain strategy — flat-pack is a SC decision first
2. Shifting assembly work to the customer can be a value proposition, not a compromise
3. Large, stable orders create mutual efficiency gains for suppliers and buyer
