## Overview

Seven-Eleven Japan (SEJ) is one of the most studied supply chain success stories — a convenience store chain that used **extremely responsive supply chain and information systems** to dominate Japan's retail market.

---

## Growth
- Total store sales: ¥1B (1974) → ¥2.7T (2016)
- Profit (2016): ¥304B
- Driven entirely by SC excellence and information superiority

---

## Key Supply Chain Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **High store density** | Stores clustered in areas → efficient replenishment, lower transport cost per delivery |
| **Very responsive replenishment** | Multiple deliveries per day (up to 3 times daily) |
| **Demand-based merchandise mix** | Product mix changed by time of day based on actual demand data |
| **Centralised DC model** | Combined delivery from multiple suppliers at DC → reduced inbound truck visits to stores |
| **Information infrastructure** | Real-time POS data → instant visibility of what is selling where |
| **Continuous product development** | New products and services constantly added (bill payment, ATM, digital printing) |

---

## Strategic Fit Analysis (Chopra)

| Functional Strategy | Alignment |
|---|---|
| **Marketing** | Emphasises convenience, variety, access |
| **New product development** | Constantly adds products exploiting information and customer traffic |
| **Operations/Distribution** | High density, responsiveness, excellent information infrastructure |

This creates a **virtuous cycle**: better SC → more products → more demand → larger network → even better SC economics.

---

## Lessons for Supply Chain Design

1. Responsiveness can be a competitive strategy, not just a cost
2. Real-time information sharing is the foundation of responsive SC
3. Product mix management by time of day = precision demand matching
4. Clustering stores around DCs reduces last-mile cost while enabling high frequency

## Compared to Sam's Club (Chopra)

| Dimension | 7-Eleven Japan | Sam's Club |
|---|---|---|
| Customer priority | Convenience, speed | Low price, bulk |
| SC strategy | Responsive, frequent delivery | Efficient, low cost |
| Inventory level | Low (multiple daily deliveries) | High (bulk purchase) |
| Competitive basis | Availability at any time | Price |
