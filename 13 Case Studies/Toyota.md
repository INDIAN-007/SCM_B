## Overview

Toyota's supply chain and production system — the **Toyota Production System (TPS)** — is the origin of Lean manufacturing and the most studied production/SC system in history.

---

## Toyota Supply Chain Structure (Chopra)

```
Raw Material Suppliers (Tier 3/4)
    ↓
Component Suppliers (Tier 2)
    ↓
Module Suppliers (Tier 1) — Electronics, Powertrain, Chassis
    ↓
Toyota Assembly Plants
    ↓
Regional Distributors
    ↓
Toyota Dealers
    ↓
Customer
```

---

## Toyota Production System Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Just-in-Time (JIT)** | Materials arrive exactly when needed — no buffer inventory |
| **Jidoka** | Machines stop automatically when defects detected — quality built-in |
| **Heijunka** | Production levelling — smooth demand across time periods |
| **Kaizen** | Continuous improvement — every employee involved |
| **Kanban** | Visual pull system — nothing produced without a signal |
| **Takt Time** | Production pace set by customer demand |
| **SMED** | Fast changeovers enable small-batch production |
| **5S** | Organised, clean workplace enables all other improvements |

---

## Toyota's Global SC Strategy (Chopra)

- Production facilities in **each major market** (strategic decision)
- Local production reduces import duties, improves responsiveness, reduces currency risk
- Hyundai (competitor) followed similar strategy — built 2nd Indian plant in 2008 → became 2nd largest + largest exporter in India by 2015

---

## Supplier Relationships

- Long-term partnerships with Tier 1 suppliers
- Joint development of components
- Transparent cost-sharing
- Suppliers located near plants (geographic clustering)
- Continuous improvement programs shared with suppliers

---

## SC Resilience — 2011 Earthquake

The 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami devastated Toyota's supply chain — demonstrating the risk of lean single-source supply chains.

**Learning:** Toyota subsequently mapped its supply chain to Tier 2/3 suppliers and required suppliers to maintain higher inventory buffers for critical components.

---

## Lessons

1. Lean SC requires extremely reliable suppliers and processes — not achievable without long-term partnerships
2. JIT reduces cost but increases fragility — need risk management alongside lean
3. Production near customers = strategic SC advantage
4. Employee involvement at every level is essential for continuous improvement
