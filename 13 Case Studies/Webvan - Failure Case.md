# Webvan — Supply Chain Failure Case

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1
> **Tags:** #casestudy #failure #ecommerce #grocery

---

## Background

Webvan was an online grocery delivery company founded in 1996, backed by $375M in VC and went public in 1999 at peak dot-com enthusiasm. It folded in 2001.

---

## The Failure Story

### What Webvan Built
- Large, automated warehouses in major US cities
- Home delivery from centrally located distribution centres
- Promised 30-minute delivery windows for groceries

### Why the SC Failed (Chopra Analysis)

| Problem | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Higher transportation cost** | Home delivery of individual orders vs full truckloads to supermarket stores — dramatically higher per-unit cost |
| **Higher labour cost** | Webvan picked individual customer orders (1.5 items/min) vs supermarkets where customers do their own picking |
| **Similar inventory turns** | Webvan turned inventory only marginally faster than supermarkets — eliminating the one potential advantage |
| **Lower density** | Delivering to scattered homes vs delivering to clustered stores |
| **Oversized fixed cost** | Massive warehouse investments amortised over insufficient volume |

### The Traditional Supermarket Advantage

| Factor | Webvan | Supermarket |
|---|---|---|
| Inbound transport | Warehouse → Home (expensive LTL) | DC → Store (cheap FTL) |
| Picking | Webvan employees pick each order | Customers pick their own orders — free labour |
| Transport cost | High (individual deliveries) | Low (full truckloads to stores) |
| Inventory turns | Marginal improvement | Strong (customers pick immediately) |

---

## Lessons

1. **Supply chain fundamentals matter more than business model novelty**
2. Shifting picking to the customer is a massive cost advantage that's easy to underestimate
3. Delivery density is the economics foundation of last-mile — Webvan's sparse order base made it unworkable
4. The SC must be designed to be cost-competitive, not just technically feasible

---

## Modern Context

Amazon failed repeatedly at grocery delivery until it:
- Acquired Whole Foods (physical network for click-and-collect)
- Built Amazon Fresh with dense urban FCs
- Developed Instacart partnerships for existing store picking

The lesson Webvan taught was eventually absorbed — but it took 20 years.
