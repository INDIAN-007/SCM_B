## Four Models — CA-M-OSA

*Author's Tip: Remember CA-M-OSA (Channel Assembly, Make-to-Order, Stock, Availability)*

---

### a. Make-to-Stock (MTS) — Integrated Model

**Trigger:** ERP-driven forecast; products stored in advance.

- Real-time demand visibility through ERP
- MRP, production plans, schedules aligned to demand
- High inventory investment
- Best for stable, predictable demand

**Examples:** P&G, HUL, Godrej (multiple channels — retail, online, canteens, supermarkets)

---

### b. Make-to-Order (MTO) — Built to Order

**Trigger:** Customer order received → build immediately.

- Fast-paced, requires high inventory planning for components
- Supports mass customisation (Lot Size of One)
- Low finished goods inventory, higher WIP component inventory
- Requires very responsive supplier network

**Examples:** Dell Computers — delivers personalised product within defined timelines

---

### c. Make-to-Availability (MTA) — Continuous Replenishment

**Trigger:** Continuous replenishment based on demand from customer segments.

- Uses Theory of Constraints (TOC) as implementation framework
- Ensures stock consistency without overstock
- Popular for industrial and infrastructure companies

**Examples:** ABB, Tata Steel, Titan, Liberty

---

### d. Channel Assembly Model (CAM)

**Trigger:** Parts/materials received at 3PL → assembled and distributed.

- Equivalent to MTS but with an added assembly leg at 3PL partner
- Reduces finished goods at central location
- Enables regional customisation (size, labelling, packaging)

**Examples:**
- Tata Salt: bulk salt shipped to 3PL partners → repackaged into 1kg pouches → distributed to dealers
- Ingram Micro: components shipped to regional 3PLs → assembled into customer-specific configs

---

## Choosing the Right Model

Consider:
1. Demand predictability (stable → MTS; variable → MTO)
2. Product customisation level
3. Lead time tolerance of customers
4. Inventory investment capacity
5. Supplier responsiveness
