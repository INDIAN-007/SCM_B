# Process Views of a Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1
> **Tags:** #foundations #process

---

## Two Complementary Views

### 1. Cycle View

Processes in a supply chain are divided into a **series of cycles** between consecutive stages.

```
Customer Order Cycle
  ↓
Replenishment Cycle
  ↓
Manufacturing Cycle
  ↓
Procurement Cycle
```

| Cycle | Between | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Customer Order Cycle | Customer ↔ Retailer | Order entry, fulfilment, delivery |
| Replenishment Cycle | Retailer ↔ Distributor | Replenishment order, supply |
| Manufacturing Cycle | Distributor ↔ Manufacturer | Production run, shipping |
| Procurement Cycle | Manufacturer ↔ Supplier | Raw material purchase, delivery |

**Best used for:** Operational decisions — identifying roles and responsibilities at each stage.

---

### 2. Push/Pull View

Processes are divided into those executed in response to a **customer order** (pull) vs those done in **anticipation of demand** (push).

| Process Type | Trigger | Horizon | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pull** | Customer order | Short (now) | Order picking, final assembly, delivery |
| **Push** | Forecast | Long (future) | Production planning, procurement, safety stock build |

**Push–Pull Boundary:** The boundary point where inventories are held and the transition from push to pull occurs.

- Dell (original): Push-pull boundary very close to customer (components pushed, final assembly pulled)
- Amazon standard products: Mostly push (stocked in FC), with pull at order fulfilment
- Custom furniture: Push for raw materials, pull all the way to final product

## Macro Processes in a Supply Chain

Three macro processes cover the entire SC:

| Macro Process | Scope | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **CRM** (Customer Relationship Management) | Firm ↔ Customer | Marketing, sales, order management, call centre |
| **ISCM** (Internal SC Management) | Within firm | Strategic planning, demand planning, capacity planning, order fulfilment |
| **SRM** (Supplier Relationship Management) | Firm ↔ Supplier | Design collaboration, sourcing, purchasing, supply collaboration |
