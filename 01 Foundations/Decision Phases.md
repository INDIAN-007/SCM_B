# Decision Phases in a Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1
> **Tags:** #foundations #strategy #planning

---

## Overview

Supply chain decisions fall into three phases based on **frequency** and **time horizon**. Each decision should raise the supply chain surplus.

---

## Phase 1 — Supply Chain Strategy / Design

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Horizon** | 3–10 years |
| **Frequency** | Infrequent, expensive to alter |
| **Nature** | Structural, long-term |

### Key Decisions
- Facility locations and capacities (plants, warehouses)
- Outsource vs in-house for each SC function
- Products manufactured / stored at which locations
- Modes of transportation available on each leg
- Type of information system (ERP, WMS, TMS)
- Supplier selection strategy

### Example
Hyundai's decision to build a second manufacturing plant in India (2008) — a strategic decision that enabled cost-effective service of the growing Indian market and export of small cars globally.

---

## Phase 2 — Supply Chain Planning

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Horizon** | Quarter to 1 year |
| **Frequency** | Regular (annual, quarterly) |
| **Nature** | Constrained by strategic decisions |

### Key Decisions
- Which markets will be supplied from which locations
- Subcontracting decisions
- Inventory policies (safety stock, cycle stock)
- Timing and size of price promotions
- Replenishment strategies

### Example
Hyundai's decision on production quantities and target markets for its two Indian plants is a planning decision. Done within the constraints set by strategic phase.

---

## Phase 3 — Supply Chain Operations

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Horizon** | Daily to weekly |
| **Frequency** | Continuous |
| **Nature** | Constrained by planning policies |

### Key Decisions
- Individual customer order allocation
- Delivery scheduling
- Picking and packing operations
- Replenishment triggers
- Short-term capacity adjustments

### Example
Order management at an Amazon fulfilment centre — which warehouse ships which order, which carrier picks up, what route is taken.

---

## Kuldeepak's Three-Level Framework

| Level | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Strategic** | Most important, long-term, high risk — e.g. ship from plant or keep stock in warehouse? |
| **Tactical** | Medium-term, moderate resources — e.g. items to store, how much to invest in holding inventory |
| **Operational** | Short-term, detailed, low risk — e.g. order size for coming month |
