# Supply Chain vs Demand Chain

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #foundations #strategy

---

## Core Distinction

- **Supply Chain** — Origin of movement triggered by the manufacturer or service provider (push logic)
- **Demand Chain** — Movement starts when there is a need from consumers (pull logic)

> "Supply Chain and Demand Chain are basically two sides of the same coin." — Kuldeepak Singh

---

## COPPER Matrix Comparison

| Parameter | Supply Chain | Demand Chain |
|---|---|---|
| **C**apital Flows | Cost drives the decision | Cash flow and profitability are important |
| **O**bjective | Focus on operational planning | Focus on demand generation from market |
| **P**eriod | Short-term goals | Long-term goals |
| **P**lanning | Focus on execution | Focus on delivering value |
| **E**fficiency | Focus on high efficiency | Focus on effectiveness |
| **R**isk | Risk averse | Calculated risk |

*Author's Tip: Remember the COPPER Matrix to quickly recall these dynamics.*

---

## Strategic Implications

| Strategy | Description | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **PUSH** | Produce based on forecast; push inventory downstream | Stable demand, FMCG |
| **PULL** | Produce only when demand signal received | Variable demand, customisation |

---

## Real Examples

| Strategy | Company | Model |
|---|---|---|
| Push | P&G, HUL, Godrej | Make-to-Stock, FMCG |
| Pull | Dell (pre-2007) | Build-to-Order |
| Hybrid | Most modern companies | Push for standard, pull for custom |
