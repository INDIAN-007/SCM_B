# Supply Chain Surplus

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.1
> **Tags:** #foundations #economics #kpi

---

## Definition

```
Supply Chain Surplus = Customer Value − Supply Chain Cost
```

- **Customer Value** — maximum amount the customer is willing to pay for the product
- **Supply Chain Cost** — total cost incurred across all stages to fulfil the request
- **Consumer Surplus** — difference between value and price, retained by customer
- **Supply Chain Profitability** — revenue minus total SC cost; shared across all stages

---

## Why It Matters

- Sole source of revenue = the customer
- All other cash flows are fund exchanges between stages
- A focus on individual stage profitability can *reduce* overall surplus
- Growing the surplus = growing the pie for all SC members

---

## Key Insight: Distributors in India vs USA

- **USA:** Consolidated retail → distributors add little value → fewer intermediaries
- **India:** Millions of small retailers → distributors break bulk, reduce transport cost, handle collections → distributors create surplus by reducing SC cost

This explains different SC structures across markets with the same logic: **does this stage grow the surplus?**

---

## Sources of Supply Chain Cost
- Procurement and raw material cost
- Manufacturing and conversion cost
- Inventory holding cost
- Transportation cost
- Warehousing cost
- Information systems cost
- Overhead and administrative cost

---

## Examples
- Best Buy sells a router for $60 → surplus = $60 minus all costs across SC stages
- GM (early 2000s) — excess inventory forced large markdowns → surplus destroyed
- Amazon — outbound shipping cost was $11.54B in 2015, net shipping loss ~19x net income → optimising this is critical to surplus
