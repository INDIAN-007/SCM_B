# Risk and Reward Sharing in Supply Chain

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.15
> **Tags:** #sourcing #contracts #incentives

---

## The Problem with Standard Contracts

Under a standard wholesale price contract:
- Supplier produces and sells at agreed price
- Buyer bears all demand risk (if demand is low, buyer is stuck with excess inventory)
- Neither party is fully incentivised to optimise total SC performance

---

## Contract Types for Risk Sharing

### Buy-Back Contract
- Supplier agrees to buy back unsold inventory at a buyback price
- Buyer orders more (reduces stockout risk) because downside is limited
- Supplier shares downside risk of overstock

### Revenue-Sharing Contract
- Buyer pays lower wholesale price in exchange for sharing a % of revenue with supplier
- Used by Blockbuster/Hollywood studios (and now streaming platforms)
- Aligns interests: both parties benefit from higher sales

### Quantity Flexibility Contract
- Supplier provides flexibility in delivered quantity within a range
- Buyer commits to a base quantity; can adjust up/down within bounds
- Reduces supplier production uncertainty

### Performance-Based Contracts (3PL / Service)
- Payment linked to outcome metrics (on-time delivery, fill rate, damage rate)
- Aligns third-party incentives with SC objectives
- Includes performance reward and penalty clauses

---

## Designing Effective Contracts

To achieve supply chain coordination:
1. Identify the source of misalignment (who bears what risk)
2. Design the contract to align marginal costs and benefits
3. Include information sharing obligations
4. Build in governance and dispute resolution
