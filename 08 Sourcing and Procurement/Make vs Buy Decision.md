# Make vs Buy Decision

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.15
> **Tags:** #sourcing #makevsbuy #outsourcing

---

## The Core Question

Should the company **perform this supply chain function in-house**, or **outsource it** to a third party?

---

## Factors Favouring In-House (Make)

- Core competency / strategic capability
- IP or trade secret involved
- Volume is large enough for economies of scale
- Quality control is critical and hard to specify
- Coordination cost of outsourcing is high
- Long-term investment in capability needed

## Factors Favouring Outsource (Buy)

- Non-core function
- Third party can do it at lower cost (economies of scale from serving many clients)
- Third party has specialised expertise
- Flexibility to scale up/down without capital commitment
- Capital can be redeployed to higher-value activities
- Supplier has better geographic reach

---

## Outsourcing Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Loss of capability | Maintain some in-house knowledge |
| Supplier dependency | Dual source; exit clauses |
| Quality degradation | SLAs with penalty; audit rights |
| Hidden costs | TCO analysis; detailed contracts |
| IP theft | NDAs; geography restrictions; limit data sharing |
| Bullwhip amplification | Share information; collaborate closely |

---

## The Third-Party Behavior Problem (Chopra Ch.15)

When a company outsources to a 3PL or supplier, the third party optimises **their own profit**, not the total SC surplus. This misalignment can hurt SC performance.

**Example:** A 3PL is paid per shipment → has incentive to make more, smaller shipments → increases cost and carbon. Solution: align incentives through performance-based contracts.
