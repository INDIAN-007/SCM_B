## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight Factors |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | Unit price, payment terms, volume discounts |
| **Quality** | Defect rate, certifications (ISO, GMP), audit results |
| **Delivery** | Lead time, on-time delivery %, OTIF rate |
| **Flexibility** | Min/max order quantities, ramp-up speed |
| **Financial stability** | Credit rating, years in business, revenue size |
| **Geographic location** | Proximity, geopolitical risk |
| **Technology capability** | EDI, API, digital collaboration readiness |
| **Sustainability** | Carbon footprint, labour practices, certifications |
| **Relationship quality** | Communication, responsiveness, willingness to collaborate |

---

## Supplier Base Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|
| **Single Source** | All volume with one supplier | Lowest cost, highest supply risk |
| **Dual Source** | Two suppliers sharing volume | Moderate cost, moderate risk |
| **Multiple Source** | 3+ suppliers competing | Highest resilience, harder to manage |
| **Preferred / Approved List** | Pre-qualified suppliers only | Balances quality and options |

---

## Supplier Development

- Long-term suppliers may need investment to meet quality, delivery, or technology standards
- Joint improvement programs (Kaizen events, Six Sigma projects)
- Transparency in sharing forecasts and capacity plans

---

## Vendor Onboarding Checklist (Kuldeepak)

- Compliance (GST, licences, labour law)
- Authenticity and legal entity verification
- Asset and manpower strength
- Segment experience and reference clients
- Credit terms and financial stability
- Rate competitiveness + SLA commitment
- Digital capability (tracking, EDI, system integration)
- Insurance and risk coverage
