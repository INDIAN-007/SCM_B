# Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

> **Source:** Chopra Ch.15
> **Tags:** #sourcing #tco #procurement #cost

---

## Definition

TCO is the **complete cost of acquiring and using a product or service** — going beyond purchase price to include all downstream costs.

---

## TCO Components

```
TCO = Purchase Price
    + Inbound Transportation & Freight
    + Receiving, Inspection & Quality
    + Inventory Holding (longer lead time = more safety stock)
    + Rework / Defect & Warranty Cost
    + Supply Disruption Risk Premium
    + Relationship & Contract Management Cost
    + Customs, Duties & Compliance
    + Currency Hedging Cost
    + End-of-life / Disposal Cost
```

---

## Why TCO Matters

- A cheaper offshore supplier may cost more in TCO due to:
  - Long lead time requiring 3x safety stock
  - Higher inspection cost (quality variation)
  - Currency risk
  - Supply disruption probability
  - Carbon tax liability

- TCO analysis reveals the **true cost** of a sourcing decision

---

## Example

| Supplier | Unit Price | Lead Time | Safety Stock Cost | Defect Rate Cost | TCO |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| China (offshore) | $10 | 8 weeks | $2 | $1.50 | $13.50 |
| Mexico (nearshore) | $13 | 2 weeks | $0.50 | $0.25 | $13.75 |
| Domestic | $16 | 1 week | $0.20 | $0.10 | $16.30 |

In this example, the cheapest unit price leads to similar TCO to nearshore — actual decision depends on strategic fit.
