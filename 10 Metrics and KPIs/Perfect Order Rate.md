# Perfect Order Rate

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #kpi #orderfulfilment #quality

---

## Definition

The Perfect Order Measure calculates the **error-free rate across every stage** of the order fulfilment process — from forecasting through delivery.

A "perfect order" is one that is:
- **On time** — delivered within promised window
- **In full** — correct quantity delivered
- **Undamaged** — no transit damage
- **Correctly invoiced** — accurate billing
- **With correct documentation** — all paperwork correct

---

## Formula (Kuldeepak)

```
Perfect Order Rate = (Total Orders − Erratically Executed Orders) / Total Orders × 100
```

### Multiplicative Method (All stages must be perfect)
```
Perfect Order Rate = % On-Time × % Complete × % Damage-Free × % Correct Invoice

Example:
  97% × 98% × 99% × 99.5% = 93.7%
```

Even if each step is 99% perfect, four steps multiply to only 96.1% — showing how stage-by-stage errors compound.

---

## Stages Tracked (Kuldeepak)

| Stage | Example Error |
|---|---|
| Order forecasting | Wrong quantity in purchase order |
| Warehouse picking | Wrong item or short-picked |
| Shipping | Late dispatch, wrong carrier |
| Invoicing | Incorrect price, missing line |

---

## Why It Matters

- Direct driver of customer satisfaction
- Measures overall supply chain execution quality
- Low perfect order rate → high customer churn, returns, disputes
- Industry leaders target >95–98%
