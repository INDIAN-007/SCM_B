# GMROI — Gross Margin Return on Investment

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #kpi #financial #inventory #profitability

---

## Definition

GMROI measures the **gross profit earned on every rupee (or dollar) invested in inventory**.

It tells you: "Which SKUs or brands generate the most profit per unit of inventory investment?"

---

## Formula (Kuldeepak)

```
GMROI = [Gross Profit] / [(Opening Stock + Closing Stock) / 2] × 100
```

Or equivalently:
```
GMROI = Gross Margin % × Inventory Turns
```

---

## Interpretation

| GMROI | Meaning |
|---|---|
| GMROI > 100% | Generating more than ₹1 of gross profit per ₹1 invested |
| GMROI < 100% | Inventory investment exceeds gross profit generated |
| Higher GMROI | Better inventory profitability |

---

## Application

- **SKU rationalisation:** Drop SKUs with consistently low GMROI
- **Assortment optimisation:** Increase shelf space for high-GMROI items
- **Buying decisions:** Evaluate new products by projected GMROI
- **Supplier negotiation:** Low GMROI on a product → renegotiate or delist

---

## GMROI vs Turn-Earn Index

| Metric | Formula | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **GMROI** | Gross Profit / Avg Inventory × 100 | Profitability per inventory ₹ |
| **Turn-Earn Index** | Inventory Turns × Gross Profit % × 100 | Balances turns and margin |

**Key difference:** TEI penalises SKUs that have high margin but low turns (or vice versa) — GMROI can mask this imbalance.

---

## Example

| SKU | Gross Profit | Avg Inventory | GMROI |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | ₹50,000 | ₹25,000 | 200% |
| B | ₹80,000 | ₹200,000 | 40% |
| C | ₹30,000 | ₹15,000 | 200% |

SKU B has higher gross profit but terrible GMROI — too much capital tied up.
