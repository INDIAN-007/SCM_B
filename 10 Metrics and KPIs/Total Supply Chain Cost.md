# Total Supply Chain Cost (TSCC)

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #kpi #cost #supply-chain

---

## Definition

Total Supply Chain Cost is calculated to understand **all end-to-end cost factors** involved — from material procurement to customer delivery.

---

## Formula (Kuldeepak)

```
TSCC = Material Cost
     + Manpower Cost
     + Inventory Holding Cost
     + Logistics Cost
     + Overheads
```

### Advanced Calculation Adds:
- Quality conformance cost (inspection, certifications)
- Delay cost (penalty for late delivery)
- Lead time cost (opportunity cost of long lead time)
- Returns processing cost
- Carbon / sustainability cost

---

## Cost Component Breakdown

| Component | What It Includes |
|---|---|
| **Material** | Raw materials, components, packaging at purchase price |
| **Manpower** | Direct labour (production, warehouse, transport) + indirect (supervision) |
| **Inventory Holding** | Capital cost + space + insurance + obsolescence + shrinkage |
| **Logistics** | Primary transport + secondary transport + last-mile |
| **Overheads** | Admin, IT systems, quality, finance, facilities maintenance |

---

## Logistics as % of GDP — Benchmark

| Country | Logistics Cost as % of GDP |
|---|---|
| Germany, USA | ~7–8% |
| China | ~14% |
| **India** | ~14% (Kuldeepak notes this is double benchmark) |

India's high logistics cost is due to:
- Road speed < 40 km/h on national highways
- Poor multimodal integration
- High return rates and compliance cost
- Fragmented logistics ecosystem

---

## Using TSCC for Decision Making

- Compare insource vs outsource options on TSCC basis, not just unit cost
- Benchmark TSCC against industry peers
- Identify the highest-cost components to drive improvement projects
- Link to supply chain profitability: SC Surplus = Customer Value − TSCC
