# Turn-Earn Index (TEI)

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #kpi #inventory #margin #balance

---

## Definition

TEI balances inventory turns and gross margin to ensure that:
- **Low-margin SKUs have high turns** (compensating via volume)
- **High-margin SKUs can afford lower turns** (profit per unit justifies slower movement)

---

## Formula (Kuldeepak)

```
TEI = Inventory Turns × Gross Profit % × 100
```

---

## Interpretation

| Scenario | What It Means |
|---|---|
| High turns + low margin = adequate TEI | Volume compensates for thin margin |
| Low turns + high margin = adequate TEI | Premium product justifies slow movement |
| Low turns + low margin = low TEI → problem | Neither volume nor margin justifies carrying this SKU |
| High turns + high margin = very high TEI → prioritise | Best performer; maximise space and attention |

---

## Example

| SKU | Inv Turns | Gross Margin % | TEI |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (staple) | 24 | 8% | 192 |
| B (premium) | 4 | 45% | 180 |
| C (slow + low margin) | 2 | 12% | 24 — problematic |

SKU C should be reviewed for delisting or renegotiation.

---

## Practical Use

- Used in retail and FMCG for assortment planning
- Helps buyers decide which SKUs to promote, expand, or delist
- Can be used to set performance targets for buyers by category
