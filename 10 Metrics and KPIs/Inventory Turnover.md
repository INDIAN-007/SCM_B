# Inventory Turnover

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.4 · Chopra Ch.3
> **Tags:** #kpi #inventory #efficiency

---

## Formula

```
Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory Value

Average Inventory = (Opening Stock Value + Closing Stock Value) / 2

Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) = 365 / Inventory Turnover
```

---

## Interpretation

| Turns | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High turns (e.g. 20+) | Fast-moving; low holding cost; lean supply chain |
| Low turns (e.g. 2) | Slow-moving; high holding cost; risk of obsolescence |

High turns = less capital tied up = better cash flow = lower holding cost.

## Industry Benchmarks

| Sector | Typical Turns |
|---|---|
| Grocery / FMCG | 20–40 |
| Consumer Electronics | 8–15 |
| Automotive | 10–15 |
| Apparel / Fashion | 4–8 |
| Pharmaceutical | 3–6 |
| Heavy Equipment | 2–4 |
| MRO / Spare Parts | 1–4 |

## Chopra Comparison (2013)
- **Amazon:** 7.31 turns (online retail + AWS)
- **Nordstrom:** 5.46 turns (brick-and-mortar retail) — impressively close to Amazon

---

## Improving Inventory Turnover

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| Reduce safety stock | Immediate but increases stockout risk |
| Improve demand forecasting | Less buffer needed |
| Shorten replenishment lead time | Less cycle stock and safety stock needed |
| Rationalise SKUs (ABC analysis) | Eliminate slow movers |
| Use cross-docking | Eliminates warehouse-level inventory |
| Shift to JIT / VMI | Supplier-managed replenishment |
