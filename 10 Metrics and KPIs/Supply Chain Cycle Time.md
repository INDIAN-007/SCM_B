# Supply Chain Cycle Time

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.1
> **Tags:** #kpi #cycletime #efficiency

---

## Definition

Supply Chain Cycle Time indicates the **overall efficiency of the supply chain** — short cycles make a more agile and responsive supply chain.

---

## Formula (Kuldeepak)

```
Supply Chain Cycle Time = Sum of the longest lead times for each stage of the cycle
```

It represents the **critical path** through the entire supply chain — the minimum time to fulfil demand if all inventory were depleted.

---

## Stages in the Cycle

```
Demand Signal
    → Procurement Lead Time (supplier to factory)
    → Manufacturing / Assembly Lead Time
    → Outbound Transportation Lead Time
    → Receiving / Warehouse Processing Time
    → Last-Mile Delivery Time
    = Total Supply Chain Cycle Time
```

---

## Example

| Stage | Lead Time |
|---|---|
| Supplier procurement | 14 days |
| Manufacturing | 5 days |
| Outbound transport | 3 days |
| Warehouse processing | 1 day |
| Last-mile delivery | 1 day |
| **Total SC Cycle Time** | **24 days** |

---

## Reducing SC Cycle Time

| Lever | Impact |
|---|---|
| Supplier development (shorter lead time) | Reduces procurement stage |
| Lean manufacturing | Reduces manufacturing stage |
| Closer supplier location | Reduces inbound transport |
| Cross-docking | Eliminates warehouse stage |
| Last-mile optimisation | Reduces delivery stage |
| Postponement strategy | Delays only final differentiation |
