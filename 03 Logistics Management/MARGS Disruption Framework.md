## MARGS — Five Disruptors

Kuldeepak's framework for key disruption factors in logistics and supply chain:

| Letter | Disruptor | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **M** | Mass Customisation | "Lot size of one" — B2O for masses driven by purchasing power and internet. Products vary in size, weight, volume → serious cost-to-serve challenges |
| **A** | Amazon Effect | New-age platforms (Amazon, Flipkart) have raised customer expectations for agile, flexible, responsive delivery at lower cost |
| **R** | Rise of Machines | Robotics, ML, AI, self-driving vehicles, 3D printing, blockchain, AR — requires new roles (CDO, CAO) but fewer humans overall |
| **G** | Green Energy | Fossil fuels finite; DHL sustainability report: serious trouble by 2040. Solar, electricity, hydrogen fuel cells in development — lower cost + lower emissions |
| **S** | Startups | $2.4B invested in Indian logistics startups in 2019. Offer technology-enabled efficiency, visibility, better decision-making — opportunity AND threat |

## Building Resilience Against MARGS

From Kuldeepak Ch.1 on resilient supply chains:

**Key questions for SC leaders:**
1. Is a disruption strategy in place?
2. How quickly can the SC be restored when disrupted?
3. Are teams trained, prepared, and empowered to handle change?

**Three pillars of resilience:**
- **Visibility** — real-time data across the supply chain
- **Flexibility** — ability to switch modes, suppliers, routes quickly
- **Agility** — alter course at lesser cost without service level disruption

> "Resilience is not only to sustain but to take correctives if disruption takes place." — Kuldeepak Singh

## Additional Disruption Factors (Kuldeepak Ch.5)

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| Compliance changes | GST, e-way bills, invoice portals — administrative burden |
| Demand fluctuations | Bullwhip effect, e-commerce spikes, shortened product lifecycles |
| Changing customer behaviour | Omnichannel expectations, post-Covid safety requirements |
| Complexity | Multi-echelon networks, high SKU count, dispersed geography |
| Sustainability | Customer pressure for green logistics, carbon reduction |
| Manpower | Unorganised sector, skills gap, high variable demand |
