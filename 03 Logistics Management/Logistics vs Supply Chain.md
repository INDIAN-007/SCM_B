# Logistics vs Supply Chain

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.2
> **Tags:** #logistics #definition

---

## Definitions

**Logistics (CSCMP):** That part of supply chain management that plans, implements, and controls the efficient, effective forward and reverse flow and storage of goods, services, and related information between the point of origin and point of consumption in order to meet customers' requirements.

**Kuldeepak:** Any event of physical flow and related activities in the supply chain. Resources include people, equipment, infrastructure, and information flow that form the logistics ecosystem.

---

## Logistics ⊂ Supply Chain

```
Supply Chain Management
└── Logistics (a subset)
    ├── Transportation
    ├── Warehousing
    ├── Inventory Management
    └── Related information flow
```

- **Logistics** = the physical and information flow of goods
- **Supply chain** = end-to-end network including suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, customers, and the strategy connecting them

| Dimension | Logistics | Supply Chain |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Narrower — flow of goods | Broader — full network strategy |
| Focus | Meeting customer requirements | Competitive advantage |
| Activities | Transport, warehouse, inventory | All of logistics + procurement, production, distribution |

---

## Logistics Segmentation (Kuldeepak)

| Basis | Examples |
|---|---|
| Industry | Telecom, Coal, Steel, Consumer Electronics, Pharma |
| Product type | Food vs Non-Food; Perishable vs Non-Perishable |
| Customer type | Retail vs Institutional (B2C vs B2B) |
| Technical requirements | Cold chain vs Normal; Hazmat vs Standard |
