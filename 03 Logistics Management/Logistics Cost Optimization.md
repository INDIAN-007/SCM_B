## The Core Challenge

Logistics is one of the biggest cost factors in supply chain. Decision-makers often focus only on vendor negotiation — but the sustainable approach is to **remove inefficiencies across the entire logistics system**.

---

## Five Levers for Optimization (Kuldeepak — Sections 2.4 a–e)

### a. Logistics Network Redesign
"Design for Logistics" from product development stage.

Three principles:
1. **Packaging** — design for safer delivery at lower cost; encourage re-use
2. **Transportation mode mix** — select optimal mode or combination
3. **Process realignment** — convert sequential processes to concurrent to reduce lead time

### b. Vendor Management
Map vendor strength vs. available capacity.

Key decisions:
- Self-managed (owned fleet) vs 3PL vs 4PL
- Capacity required vs. vendor capacity — account for seasonality, skewness, disruption risk

**Onboarding Checklist for Vendors:**
- Compliance and authenticity
- Asset, financial, and manpower strength
- Segment strength and clientele track record
- Credit terms
- Rate competitiveness and service level commitment
- Digital collaboration capability

### c. Service Level Design
- **Mix:** Owned (40%) : Fixed-lease (40%) : On-call (20%) — sample ratio
- **Payment structure:** Advance ratio / Completion pay / Performance reward-penalty
- **Transit time categories:**
  - Maximum, Minimum, Optimistic, Pessimistic, Normal, Optimal

> **Note:** Transit time ≠ Lead time. Transit time = travel time between origin and destination. Lead time = planning to serve time (transit time is a subset).

### d. Activity-Based Cost Breakdown
Break transportation into sub-activities: Receipt, Unloading, Storage, Picking, Loading, Documentation, Returns.
Use **Activity Based Costing (ABC)** + **Pareto Analysis** to find the top cost drivers.

### e. Responsiveness and Flexibility — SHABD Method
**SHABD (Single Hour Arrangement Based on Demand):** Measures how quickly the logistics system can respond to demand changes without impacting existing throughput.

**DIGROM Implementation Framework:**
- **D**emand Assessment (seasonality, schemes, special events)
- **I**dentify the Gap (vendor capacity vs demand spike)
- **G**ross Requirement Review (transport mix, zero-based planning)
- **R**FQ / Negotiation / **O**nboarding
- **M**easure to Manage (performance review → repeat cycle)

## Cost Metrics

| Metric | Formula |
|---|---|
| Avg Transport Cost/km | Total Transport Cost / Total km |
| Cost-to-Sales Ratio | Transport Cost / Net Sales × 100 |
| Fuel vs Vehicle Running Cost | Fuel Cost / Total Vehicle Running Cost × 100 |
