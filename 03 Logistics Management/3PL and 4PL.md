# 3PL and 4PL

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.2
> **Tags:** #logistics #outsourcing #3pl #4pl

---

## Party Definitions

| Party | Who They Are |
|---|---|
| **1PL** | The company itself — owns and manages its own logistics |
| **2PL** | Asset-based carrier hired by the company (trucking company, airline) |
| **3PL** | Third Party Logistics provider — manages logistics operations on behalf of company |
| **4PL** | Fourth Party Logistics — manages the entire supply chain, including managing 3PLs |

---

## 3PL — Third Party Logistics

A 3PL executes logistics operations: transportation, warehousing, customs clearance, distribution.

### Services Offered
- Freight transportation (road, air, sea)
- Warehousing and distribution
- Value-added services (kitting, labelling, packing)
- Customs brokerage
- Reverse logistics

### When to Use 3PL
- When logistics is not a core competency
- When capital investment in assets is undesirable
- When geographic reach beyond own network is needed
- When seasonality requires flexible capacity

### Examples
- DHL, FedEx (as 3PL), Blue Dart, Mahindra Logistics, Ecom Express

---

## 4PL — Fourth Party Logistics

A 4PL takes full accountability for managing the entire supply chain on behalf of the client — designing, managing, and optimising it. The 4PL manages multiple 3PLs.

### Characteristics
- Single point of accountability for the client
- Technology-centric (provides visibility, analytics, optimisation)
- Asset-light — manages assets of 3PLs
- Strategic partner, not just executer

### Examples
- Accenture Supply Chain, IBM SCM Services, Tech Mahindra IDS&SP

---

## 3PL vs 4PL Comparison

| Dimension | 3PL | 4PL |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Executes logistics tasks | Designs and manages entire SC |
| Assets | May own assets | Generally asset-light |
| Accountability | Operational | Strategic + operational |
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Relationship | Transactional | Partnership |

## Onboarding a Vendor (Kuldeepak Checklist)
- Compliance verification (GST, licences)
- Authenticity and legal status
- Asset strength (vehicles, warehouses, equipment)
- Financial stability
- Segment experience and clientele
- Credit terms
- Rate competitiveness + service level commitment
- Digital capability (tracking, EDI, API integration)
