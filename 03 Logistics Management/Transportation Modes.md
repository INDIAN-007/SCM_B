# Transportation Modes

> **Source:** Kuldeepak Ch.2 · Chopra Ch.14
> **Tags:** #logistics #transportation #modes

---

## Four Primary Modes (Kuldeepak)

1. **Road** (Truck, Rail, Pipeline)
2. **Water** (Waterways, Shipping)
3. **Air** (Flights, Drones)
4. **Hybrid / Intermodal** — combination: Piggyback, TOFC, COFC, RORO, Fishy Back, Birdy Back

---

## Comparative Analysis

| Mode | Cost | Speed | Reliability | Reach / Flexibility | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Air** | Very High | Very High | High | Low (airports only) | Urgent, high-value, perishable |
| **Road (Truck)** | Medium | Medium | Medium–High | Very High | Door-to-door, flexible, last mile |
| **Rail** | Low | Medium | High | Medium (rail network) | Bulk, heavy, long distance |
| **Water (Ocean/Inland)** | Very Low | Very Low | Medium | Low–Medium | International bulk, containers |
| **Pipeline** | Low (fixed cost) | Continuous | Very High | Very Low (fixed route) | Oil, gas, chemicals |

---

## Intermodal / Hybrid Options

| Term | Description |
|---|---|
| **TOFC** (Trailer on Flat Car) | Truck trailer loaded on railway flatcar |
| **COFC** (Container on Flat Car) | ISO container on railway flatcar |
| **RORO** (Roll On Roll Off) | Vehicles driven on/off ship |
| **Piggyback** | Truck carried on rail or ship |
| **Fishy Back** | Truck + ship combination |
| **Birdy Back** | Truck + air combination |

## Mode Selection Framework (Kuldeepak)

> **Author's Tip:** Give weightage to your top factors aligned with your business USP, then work through priorities.

**Example for Consumer Products (Retail):**
1. Priority 1 — Less Lead Time → Options: Air, Rail, Road
2. Priority 2 — Low Cost → Remaining: Rail, Road
3. Priority 3 — High Reach → **Final Decision: Road**

## Transportation Infrastructure (Chopra Ch.14)

- Governments play a significant role in building and regulating transport infrastructure
- Infrastructure quality directly impacts SC responsiveness and cost
- India's logistics cost as % of GDP: ~14% (double the global benchmark of ~7-8%) partly due to infrastructure constraints
- Average road speed in India: <40 km/h on national highways — a bottleneck

---

## Tailored Transportation (Chopra)

Using different transport options for different customer segments:
- High-value, time-sensitive customers → air or express
- Bulk, cost-sensitive customers → rail or ocean
- Nearby customers → direct truck
- Distant, infrequent customers → consolidated shipments
